# HELLZAPOPPIN' LIVE! at The Loading Dock
## Scene Breakdown — Production Draft

**Series:** Hellzapoppin' No. 4
**Format:** 9:16 vertical, social media
**Target runtime:** 0:45–1:00
**Production:** a][ productions × Claude

---

## THE THROUGH LINE

We are backstage. The banner tells us this *is* the show. Nobody backstage knows that except the Curtain. The Curtain has been watching all of this for a very long time and finds it genuinely funny.

---

## SCENE 1 — THE BANNER
**~0:00–0:06**

Open on the hand-painted canvas banner filling most of the frame. Paint cans and brushes on the floor beside it, as if recently finished. It reads:

> **HELLZAPOPPIN' LIVE!**
> *at The Loading Dock*

The Janitor walks past it without a glance, mop and bucket in hand. He does not read it. He does not care. He has places to be.

*The banner stays in frame a beat after he passes. We and the Curtain share a secret.*

---

## SCENE 2 — THE PRODUCER'S OFFICE
**~0:06–0:18**

The Janitor passes a small backstage office — a door ajar, light spilling out. He slows. Peeks in.

Inside, two Producers in period-appropriate suits, papers everywhere, clearly stressed.

**PRODUCER 1:**
*"We need better material. Something people actually want to see."*

**PRODUCER 2:**
*"The curtain's the one thing that always works."*

The Janitor watches this for one beat. Shakes his head slowly. Moves on.

*He has no idea how right Producer 2 is. Neither does Producer 2.*

---

## SCENE 3 — THE SLAPSTICK SEQUENCE
**~0:18–0:40**

The Janitor returns to his work. This is where we live for a while.

**Gag 1 — The Mop:**
He sets the mop against the wall. Turns away to position the bucket. Turns back. The mop is gone. He stares at the empty wall. Looks around. Nothing. A beat of genuine puzzlement. He shrugs. Goes back to the bucket.

*At the edge of frame, barely visible: the hem of the Curtain.*

**Gag 2 — The Buckets:**
He turns back to find not one bucket but three. Arranged neatly. He stares at them. Looks around. Nobody. He shakes his head — same head shake as the office, the universal gesture of a man who has stopped asking questions. He begins navigating around the buckets.

*The Curtain's hem is visible again. Just barely.*

**Gag 3 — The Door:**
While managing his bucket situation, the Janitor drifts toward a door at the back of the space. Just a door. He reaches for the handle.

**THE CURTAIN (offstage, sudden and forceful):**
*"No!"*

The Janitor freezes.

**THE CURTAIN (settling back, almost conversational):**
*"...Not that one, friend. The last fellow who went through is still performing."*

The Janitor processes this for exactly one beat. Nods slowly — like this explains something. Picks up two of the extra buckets and carries them offscreen without another word.

---

## SCENE 4 — THE COMPLAINER
**~0:40–0:52**

No cut. No pause. Almost immediately after the Janitor exits, the Complainer enters from the other side — a paying customer, period-dressed, aggrieved, already talking.

**THE COMPLAINER:**
*"Excuse me — who do I talk to about a refund? I've seen better shows at— hello? This isn't even a stage, this is a— hello?"*

They look around, confused. The space is empty. Except for the banner. Except for the Curtain.

**THE CURTAIN (warm, unctuous, hospitable):**
*"Right this way. I think you'll find exactly what you're looking for."*

The Complainer, mollified, heads toward the door. Opens it. Steps through.

A beat.

**THE COMPLAINER (from behind the closed door):**
*"No!"*

Muffled applause surges through the door, then is cut off as it swings shut.

---

## SCENE 5 — THE SNICKER
**~0:52–1:00**

For the first time, we see the Curtain's face. Just a moment. Just enough.

It's snickering.

*Cut to black.*

---

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**The Curtain's voice:** Not booming. Older than authority. Slightly amused at all times, like it has seen every version of this night play out and finds it funny every single time. One exception: the "No!" at the door — forceful, sudden, the voice of something that doesn't usually need to raise itself. The contrast is the character.

**The Curtain's face:** First visible feature in the entire series. Should feel like a reveal that recontextualizes everything — it has always had a face, it just hasn't shown us until now. Not monstrous. Knowing.

**The banner:** Must stay accessible in at least one shot beyond the opening. It's the secret the whole piece is built on.

**The Producers:** Background texture only after their scene. Murmuring, shuffling papers. They think they're running things. They are set dressing.

**The Janitor:** Never frightened, never curious for long. A man who has made peace with not understanding his workplace. We like him. He gets out clean.

**The Complainer:** Not a character. A punchline that's been waiting since the first frame. Enough dialogue to establish type, no more.

**The door:** Never show what's on the other side. The "No!" and the applause are enough. The imagination fills in something better than we could generate.

## THEMATIC NOTES

Every character in this piece is correct about something they completely misunderstand. The Producers are right about the Curtain. The Complainer is right that it's a show. The Janitor is right to stay away from that door. Nobody has the full picture except the Curtain.

And us. We were never outside.

The banner told us at the top. The snicker confirms it at the end.
