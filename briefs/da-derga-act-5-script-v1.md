# Da Derga's Hostel — Act 5 Shooting Script v1
## "The Destruction and the Accounting"

---

## Act-level notes

**Estimated runtime:** 5:30 – 6:30.

**Aspect ratio:** 16:9 throughout. Talking head (Movement 5) noted in spine doc as a possible vertical-aspect candidate — drafted as 16:9 with options for vertical break described in shot notes. Recommendation: keep 16:9 unless Act 3's Cailb shot is committed to vertical, in which case the talking head becomes the second formal vertical break and the two doom-faces of the piece (Cailb's recognition, Conaire's accounting) are aspect-twinned.

**Voices in this act:**
- **Narrator** — returns to prominence. Carries the structural beats: the fires named, the rivers named, the cairn counted. The narrator was *small* in Act 4; here he is the structural backbone again. Same voice, but the register has shifted — quieter, more elegiac. He is not explaining now. He is counting.
- **Mac cecht** — debuts. Two brief lines, both during the water-quest. Practical, exhausted, the voice of a man who has been running for hours. ElevenLabs casting: a voice that sounds like it has been used too hard.
- **The talking head (Conaire's only speech in the piece)** — debuts at Movement 5. The praise-poem to Mac cecht. Verbatim Stokes. Speaking from a severed head; the voice should be small, dry, intimate — not theatrical. Conaire's voice is what the king sounded like before the kingship; what we hear is the voice the kingship was carrying.
- **No Thread 1.** Silent throughout the entire act. The line "Clouds of weakness are coming to you" is not heard in Act 5. The doom is no longer coming.
- **No recognition formulas.** They have done their work.
- **Ingcél, Fer rogain, Lomna** — silent in Act 5. Lomna's voice falls with his head; the other two never speak again.

**Music:**
- **Silence is the bed.** Act 5 strips out the continuous audio elements of the prior acts (Act 1's held tone, Acts 2–3's pulse, Act 4's low Suno track). What plays is intermittent — the sounds of specific actions, with clean silence between.
- **One Suno track in the entire act:** the water-quest music (Movement 4). Sparse, breathing, the sound of running. Plays once across the named-river catalog. Ends when Mac cecht finds water.
- **Wind** is the underlying constant — varies in intensity, but present from start of act to end. The world is finally outside the Hostel and the wind has nothing to obstruct it.

**Reference images required:**
- **REF #4 (severed head pieta — Mac cecht cradling Conaire's head, golden cup, water into wound, Caravaggio reference)** already established and approved.
- **REF-W:** The Hostel burning. The exterior, doors broken open, smoke and flame visible through the gaps. Distant, not full-frame. We do not get close to the burning.
- **REF-X:** Lomna's severed head. Mid-flight. We see it crossing the threshold of a doorway — caught mid-air. The eyes open, the mouth open, but no sound. Same face as REF-S, now severed.
- **REF-Y:** A dry riverbed. Stones where water should be. The river has hidden. This composition repeats with variations across the water-quest — same shape, different rivers.
- **REF-Z:** Uaran Garad — the single spring where Mac cecht finds water. A small pool, almost hidden between rocks. Reflective. The opposite of the dry riverbeds — what the cataloging has finally come to.
- **REF-AA:** The cairn at dawn. Same composition as Act 4's REF-U, but now some stones have been removed. The pile is lower, asymmetrical. The remaining stones mark the count.

**Continuity from Act 4:**
- Act 4 ended on Conaire's face inside the Hostel, raising his head — hearing the host advancing.
- Act 4 closed to black, held three seconds.
- Act 5 opens on the first fire.

## Movement 1 — The first fire

The attack begins. Source structure: fires lit and extinguished, three times.

### Shot 5.1
**Visual:** Black, holding. Then — a single point of light. Small, growing. A torch being kindled in someone's hand. We see only the flame at first, then the hand, then the hand belongs to a reaver.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Silence, then the sound of fire taking — the slow whoosh of dry wood catching.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *They lit the first fire.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 89, paraphrased. The fires lit and extinguished × 3 is the source's structural rhythm of the attack.

**Production notes:** The first fire is small, deliberate, controlled. This is not yet chaos. Veo: a single hand bringing a torch to a pile of straw or thatch at the wall of the Hostel.

### Shot 5.2
**Visual:** The Hostel exterior, the wall where the torch was placed. Flame begins to climb up the wood. Catching.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Fire growing. Wind.

### Shot 5.3
**Visual:** Inside the Hostel. The wall opposite — smoke is coming through the joints. A defender (an unnamed retainer, near Mac cecht's position) sees it. He moves toward it with a vessel of water.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Inside sound — the gathering is still nominally a gathering, but the murmur has gone tense. Voices have begun to lower. Footsteps quicken.

### Shot 5.4
**Visual:** Outside again. The fire on the wall — being doused from inside. A spray of water comes through a gap in the boards. The flame falters, dies.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** The hiss of water on flame. Then silence.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *And those within put it out.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 89.

### Shot 5.5
**Visual:** Wide. The Hostel exterior. From a high angle. The reavers around it, indistinct. Smoke rising from the place where the first fire was extinguished. The doors are still closed.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Wind. Distant voices — the reavers regrouping. No music.

## Movement 2 — Lomna and the head

The interior breach. Lomna's six crossings. Fires two and three interleaved.

### Shot 5.6
**Visual:** A side door of the Hostel — one of the seven. A blow against it from outside. The wood shudders.

**Duration:** 2s.

**Audio:** A single heavy impact.

### Shot 5.7
**Visual:** Another blow. The wood splits. A gap appears.

**Duration:** 2s.

**Audio:** Wood breaking.

### Shot 5.8
**Visual:** The gap is wide enough. A man crashes through — Lomna Drúth (REF-S). Through the broken wood. Inside the Hostel for the first time. His face is the face from Act 4 — empty, set, the grief gone.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** His entry — wood scraping. His breath. The Hostel interior noise rises around him — alarmed voices, shouts.

**Production notes:** Lomna's entry into the Hostel is the doomer crossing the threshold he wept over. The structural payoff of Act 4's emotional arc.

### Shot 5.9
**Visual:** Inside. Lomna takes one step. His sword is in his hand but he does not raise it. A blow comes from his side, off-frame — a sword's edge. We do not see it land. We see Lomna's face as it hits.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** The strike — a single heavy sound.

**Production notes:** The beheading is offscreen. We see his face in the instant before the strike connects. Veo: hold his face; the strike is audio only. This is structurally important — we are not making spectacle of the doomer's death. The accounting is what matters.

### Shot 5.10 *(Lomna's head — first crossing, OUT)*
**Visual:** **REF-X.** Lomna's severed head in the air. Mid-flight. Caught at the threshold of the broken door — half inside, half outside. The eyes are open. The mouth is open. There is no sound from it.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** The whump of the throw. Wind. Then silence.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *His own head crossed the threshold.*

**Source:** Stokes — "It will be flung into the Hostel thrice, and thrice will it be flung forth" (Stokes p. 67, said by Lomna himself in Act 4). Lomna foretold this in the source; he is now living through it. The first crossing is OUT — the defenders inside have thrown it out to his own side.

**Production notes:** **The signature shot of Act 5's middle.** REF-X is the canonical image — head caught in mid-air at the threshold. Veo: this is achievable as a held composition with subtle motion. Generate as a still with subtle motion-blur applied; the head should read as suspended, mid-throw, not floating. The face must be Lomna's (REF-S), now severed. Content moderation risk: moderate. Severed heads were workable in the Conaire-head Veo test from the seed. Should be achievable here.

### Shot 5.11
**Visual:** The head lands in the dirt outside the Hostel. Among the feet of the reavers. They look down at it. One of them recognizes it.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** The thud of impact. Silence after.

### Shot 5.12 *(Lomna's head — second crossing, IN)*
**Visual:** A different reaver — face unseen — picks up the head. He throws it back. We see the head in flight again, crossing the threshold the other direction. **REF-X composition reversed.**

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** The whump of the throw. The head hitting wood inside.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *And was flung back in.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 67.

### Shot 5.13 *(Fire 2 begins, interleaved)*
**Visual:** Outside, different wall of the Hostel. Multiple torches now. The reavers' attack has escalated — they are setting fires along the length of the building. Flame climbing.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Fire growing. Many footsteps. Shouts.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *They lit the second fire.*

### Shot 5.14 *(Lomna's head — third crossing, OUT)*
**Visual:** Inside the Hostel. The head is on the floor, where it landed from Shot 5.12. A defender — not named, not visible by face — kicks it back out through a different doorway. The head crosses again.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** The scrape of the kick. The thud of crossing.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *It crossed again.*

### Shot 5.15 *(Fire 2 extinguished)*
**Visual:** The second fire — being put out from inside. Same gesture as Shot 5.4 but the gestures are getting more frantic now. Water through gaps. The flame dies but slower this time.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Water on flame. The defenders' breath audible — they are tiring.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *And those within put it out.*

### Shot 5.16 *(Lomna's head — fourth crossing, IN)*
**Visual:** Outside. The head is on the ground again. A reaver — Fer rogain this time, briefly identifiable (REF-R) — picks it up. He looks at it for one beat. Then he throws it back in.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Wind. The throw.

**Production notes:** Fer rogain's brief identification here is important — his own brother's head is in his hands and he keeps the violence going. This is the structural completion of his Act 4 arc. He loved Conaire; he is doing this anyway; he is now handling his foster-brother's head as a thing.

### Shot 5.17 *(Fire 3 begins)*
**Visual:** The Hostel from outside, wider. Three separate fires now, on different walls. The defenders inside cannot reach all of them.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Fire louder, multiple sources. Wind carrying smoke.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *They lit the third fire.*

### Shot 5.18 *(Lomna's head — fifth crossing, OUT)*
**Visual:** Inside the Hostel. The head is again on the floor — different position. By now it is bloodied, dirtied. It does not look like Lomna anymore; it looks like an object. A foot kicks it; it skids across the floor and out through a broken board.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** The scrape. The thud.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *Fifth.*

**Production notes:** The narrator's diction has shortened — by the fifth crossing, the formula is broken. Just numbers. The accounting accelerates.

### Shot 5.19 *(Fire 3 extinguished — barely)*
**Visual:** One of the three fires being extinguished from inside. The other two are still burning. The defenders cannot keep up.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Water on flame, but the fire is bigger now — the hiss is louder, longer.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *And those within put out one of them.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 89 — by the third attempt, the defenders cannot extinguish all the fires. The Hostel begins to burn.

### Shot 5.20 *(Lomna's head — sixth crossing, IN — the final one)*
**Visual:** Outside. The head is in the dirt. Nobody picks it up cleanly this time — a foot kicks it back through the broken doorway, almost incidentally. The sixth crossing is no longer a thrown gesture; it is the head being moved by the violence around it. It is now a piece of debris.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** The scrape. The thud. The fire's roar underneath.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *Sixth. The head was not Lomna's any longer.*

**Source:** "It was not Lomna's any longer" is authored. The structural point of the head's six crossings is that violence makes the doomer into an object; we name the moment that completes.

### Shot 5.21
**Visual:** The Hostel from outside. Most of the building is now burning. The walls are still standing, but flame is in the windows, smoke from the rooftree. The reavers have pulled back to a perimeter — they are no longer attacking; they are watching it burn.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** Fire. Wind. No voices.

**Production notes:** REF-W. The Hostel burning. Established here. We do not go closer than middle distance. The destruction is visible but contained.

## Movement 3 — The thirst

The pivot. Conaire asks for water. The river hides.

### Shot 5.22
**Visual:** Inside. The Hostel is full of smoke. Bodies on the floor — not closely framed, indistinct in the smoke. Mac cecht (REF-P) is at the central hearth. Conaire (REF-A) is beside him, on his feet, but unsteady. The fire from outside is visible through breaks in the walls.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** The fire's roar is loud here but muffled by walls. Coughing. Mac cecht's breath. Conaire breathes raggedly — for the first time the audience hears him breathing.

**Production notes:** Conaire's breathing is the audio of his interior — the audience has heard him not speak for nearly 20 minutes. Now they hear his breath. The breath is dry. He needs water.

### Shot 5.23
**Visual:** Close on Conaire's face. Sweat. Smoke in his eyes. He turns to Mac cecht. His mouth opens. He still does not speak — but his hand reaches, opens. The gesture is *give me water*.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** His breath. Mac cecht beside him.

**Source:** Stokes p. 90 — Conaire's thirst is the source's pivot. The destruction has been escalating; Conaire asks for water; the rivers refuse him.

### Shot 5.24
**Visual:** A vessel is brought — a wooden cup. A cupbearer (unnamed retainer) holds it. He has come from a side room where the water is stored. He upends the cup. Nothing comes out.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** The cup, empty, against his palm.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *The water was gone from the cups.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 90, paraphrased.

### Shot 5.25
**Visual:** A different cupbearer — running from a different side room. He carries a cauldron. He upends it. A few drops fall, then nothing.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** The few drops.

### Shot 5.26
**Visual:** Inside the Hostel — a different room. We are seeing a corner the audience has not seen before. There is a small channel cut into the floor — a stream that runs through the building (Stokes specifies the Hostel was built over a river). The channel is dry. Bone-dry. Not a trace of moisture.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Silence. The fire's roar is distant here.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *The river that ran through the Hostel had hidden itself.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 90, verbatim. The Hostel was famously built over a river; the river goes underground / vanishes during the destruction. This is one of the source's strangest images and the structural reason for Mac cecht's quest.

**Production notes:** Veo can deliver this — a stone channel through a floor, dry, bone-dry. Important: it should look like a place where water *should be* and is not. Stones at the edges of the channel that are visibly wet-stained, dry now.

### Shot 5.27
**Visual:** Back at the hearth. Conaire's face. He looks at Mac cecht. The gesture again — *find water*.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** Breath. Fire.

### Shot 5.28
**Visual:** Mac cecht. Close. He looks at the king. He nods once. He turns. He is going.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** His footsteps starting.

## Movement 4 — Mac cecht's water-quest

The catalog of rivers. Structural rhyme to Act 4's chamber catalog. One Suno track, sparse, plays across this movement.

### Shot 5.29
**Visual:** Mac cecht running. Wide shot. The Hostel behind him, burning at the horizon. Open land before him. He carries the king's golden cup. He is running flat-out.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** The Suno water-quest track enters — sparse breath sounds, low strings, a sense of forward motion without rhythm. His footsteps. His breathing.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *Mac cecht ran from the Hostel with the king's cup.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 91, paraphrased.

### Shot 5.30
**Visual:** **REF-Y. First dry riverbed.** Mac cecht arrives at the bank. Stones where water should be. He stops. He looks. He turns and runs on.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Music continues. His arrival, his pause, his departure.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *He went to the Boyne. The Boyne had hidden.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 91, paraphrased. Stokes catalogs the specific rivers Mac cecht searches.

**Production notes:** The dry riverbed composition is the visual signature of this movement. REF-Y is the canonical image; each subsequent riverbed should be a variation on the same composition — same general shape (bank, channel, sky beyond), different specific stones and topography. Veo can generate variations from the same prompt structure.

### Shot 5.31
**Visual:** Mac cecht arriving at a different river. Different topography. Same emptiness.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Music. His breath audibly more labored.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *He went to the Shannon. The Shannon had hidden.*

### Shot 5.32
**Visual:** A lake. The shore. Mac cecht at the edge. The lake is empty — dry mud, cracked, where water should be.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Music. Wind across the dry lake.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *He went to Lough Ree. The Lough was empty.*

### Shot 5.33
**Visual:** Mac cecht running at night now. The light has changed. He has been running for hours. He is staggering slightly.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Music. His footsteps are heavier.

### Shot 5.34
**Visual:** Another dry riverbed. He arrives. He looks. He does not stop this time — he keeps running through, across the dry channel, up the other bank, on.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Music. His running continues without pause.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *He went to the Erne. He went to the Suir. He went to the Slaney. They had all hidden from him.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 91, compressed. The source lists many more rivers; we compress to a representative few.

**Production notes:** The pacing of the catalog accelerates — early rivers got a full shot each; the middle catalog runs four rivers across one shot with continuous footage. The narrator's diction also accelerates, four names in a single sentence.

### Shot 5.35
**Visual:** Mac cecht stops. The light is changing again — dawn approaching. He is breathing hard. The cup is still in his hand. He has been running all night.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Music thinning. His breath very labored. Wind.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *And at last —*

### Shot 5.36
**Visual:** **REF-Z.** A small pool between rocks. Almost hidden. Uaran Garad. The water catches the first light of dawn.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** Music resolves on a held chord. Then thins to nothing. The sound of water — for the first time in the entire act, the sound of water. A small spring, alive.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *— he came to a spring called Uaran Garad. The spring had not hidden.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 92. Uaran Garad is the named spring in the source where Mac cecht finally finds water.

**Production notes:** The first sound of water in Act 5 is structurally important. The destruction has been bone-dry — fires, smoke, ash, no water. The reappearance of water is the first non-destruction sound the audience has heard since Movement 1. Veo: a small reflective pool between rocks, dawn light, water visibly catching the light. Should be achievable.

### Shot 5.37
**Visual:** Mac cecht kneeling at the spring. He fills the king's golden cup. He cups his hand into the water once for himself, drinks, then stands. He has water. He turns to run back.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** Water. His drinking. His footsteps starting again.

**Production notes:** A small detail: he drinks for himself before running back. The source has this. Mac cecht is exhausted; he is human; he drinks. Then he serves his king. The order matters — he is allowed his own thirst before the king's.

## Movement 5 — The return, the head, the silence

The climax. Mac cecht returns to the Hostel. The destruction is complete. The talking head.

### Shot 5.38
**Visual:** Mac cecht running, with full cup. Dawn light. He approaches the Hostel from the open land. The Hostel is now smoking ruin — walls partly collapsed, smoke rising from the wreckage, the fires burned themselves out.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** His footsteps. His breath. Wind. The Hostel is silent now — the destruction is over. He is the only living motion in the landscape.

### Shot 5.39
**Visual:** He arrives at the broken doorway. He looks in. His face changes.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** His breath stopping.

### Shot 5.40
**Visual:** Inside the Hostel. Wide. Bodies. Many bodies. The Hostel is full of the dead. We do not see them individually — they are a field of indistinct shapes in the smoke and ash. The central hearth is still glowing. Beside the hearth — Conaire's body. Headless.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** Silence. Wind through broken walls. No music. No voices.

**Production notes:** This is the only wide shot of the destruction's aftermath inside the Hostel. We do not linger on bodies; the field of dead is a single composition, indistinct. The audience's eye finds Conaire's body because the camera leads there. The headlessness is shown — but plainly, without lingering. Veo: prompt for a smoky interior with multiple indistinct fallen figures, central hearth still glowing, one specific body at center foreground that is missing its head. Content moderation may push back; fall back to silhouettes if needed.

### Shot 5.41
**Visual:** Mac cecht moves into the Hostel. He passes through smoke. He is looking for something specific.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** His footsteps on debris.

### Shot 5.42
**Visual:** He finds it. The head. Conaire's severed head, on the floor, near the central hearth. The face is intact. Eyes closed. The expression — quiet.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Mac cecht kneeling. His breath.

**Production notes:** Conaire's head must be recognizable as Conaire (REF-A). Veo: the head on the floor, eyes closed, peaceful. This is the precursor to REF #4 (the pieta).

### Shot 5.43
**Visual:** Wider — Mac cecht's POV expanded. Just beyond Conaire's head, a body. The beheader. Mac cecht's eye finds him.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** Silence.

### Shot 5.44
**Visual:** Mac cecht standing. He sets the cup down beside Conaire's head. He draws his weapon. He moves toward the beheader.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** His footsteps. His weapon being drawn.

### Shot 5.45
**Visual:** The beheader does not have time to rise. Mac cecht's blow lands. We see this in middle distance, not close — a single decisive strike. The beheader falls.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** The strike. The fall.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *He killed the one who had killed the king.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 95, paraphrased.

**Production notes:** The killing is not lingered on. Single shot, plain, decisive. Mac cecht is not in rage — he is doing what needs to be done before he gets back to the head.

### Shot 5.46
**Visual:** Mac cecht returns to Conaire's head. He kneels again. He picks up the head — gently — and places it on his thigh. He picks up the golden cup, which has been sitting in the dust beside.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** His movements. The slight clink of the cup. Otherwise silence.

### Shot 5.47 *(POSSIBLE VERTICAL ASPECT BREAK — TWIN TO 3.13)*
**Visual:** **REF #4 — established.** Caravaggio pieta composition. Mac cecht cradling Conaire's severed head. Golden cup in his other hand. He is about to pour. Light falls from above — dawn through the broken rooftree.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** A single held tone enters — low, distinct from the Cailb / Badbs tone (which was high). This is a *bass* tone. The opposite end of the supernatural marker register. The same structural function — *something is happening that the world does not normally permit.*

**Production notes:** **Possible vertical-aspect break.** REF #4 was generated 16:9 but the composition is fundamentally vertical (figure cradling figure, light from above). If Cailb's shot (3.13) is committed to vertical, this becomes its formal twin — the two moments of recognition that depart aspect ratio. The case is strong: Cailb's was *judgment* on the king; this is the *accounting* of the king. They twin structurally; they should twin formally.

If 16:9: hold the pieta composition centered, dark borders carrying the weight, light from above creating a strong vertical within a horizontal frame.

### Shot 5.48
**Visual:** Closer. Mac cecht pours water from the golden cup into the wound where Conaire's head was severed. The water enters the neck. Some spills.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** The pour. The water entering flesh.

**Source:** Stokes p. 96. This is the source's strangest image and the central act of the story's resolution. The water Mac cecht ran across all of Ireland to find is given to the king through the wound that killed him.

**Production notes:** Veo's earlier test with the talking head was partial success. This shot is the precursor — the pour itself. Achievable. The composition holds the pieta; the gesture is the addition.

### Shot 5.49 *(THE TALKING HEAD — CONAIRE'S ONLY SPEECH)*
**Visual:** Close on Conaire's face. The head. Mac cecht's hands still cradling it. The eyes open.

**Duration:** 12s.

**Audio:** The bass tone holds underneath. Then his voice.

**VO (Conaire's head, on-screen, archaic — Conaire's only line in the piece):**
> *Good would I be to far-renowned Mac cecht, were I alive.*
>
> *A good man.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 96, verbatim. (Stokes's praise-poem from the head is longer in some manuscripts; the load-bearing lines are these.)

**Production notes:** **The verbal climax of the piece.** Conaire has been silent for the entire piece — over twenty minutes of silence. The audience has heard him breathe (5.22) and not speak. Now they hear him speak, but from a severed head. The voice must be small, dry, intimate. Not theatrical. ElevenLabs casting: a voice that sounds like the king Conaire might have been, now reduced to the necessary minimum. The speech is praise — the head's gift to Mac cecht is recognition.

The Veo test from the seed indicated partial success — Veo's world model defaulted to Mac cecht speaking. Production strategy:
- **Primary**: head's lips move subtly. Eyes open. Voice carries. Veo: prompt explicitly for "severed head, eyes opening, lips moving slowly to form words, no body, head cradled in another man's hands." Multiple takes.
- **Fallback 1**: close-crop the severance — frame so we cannot see whether it is severed or not, lips clearly moving on the head's face. The audience knows the severance from prior shots.
- **Fallback 2**: hold on Mac cecht's face, VO of the head's words carried as if heard by him. This is the lowest-quality fallback but preserves the climax.

The two lines should have a clear beat between them. "Good would I be to far-renowned Mac cecht, were I alive." [breath] "A good man." The second line is almost an afterthought — a quieter, almost private addition. The dignity is in its smallness.

### Shot 5.50
**Visual:** Hold on the head. The eyes are still open. Then, slowly, they close. The lips do not move again.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** The bass tone fades. Silence.

**Source:** Stokes p. 96 — the head speaks once, then is still.

**Production notes:** The audience has heard Conaire speak once. They will not hear him again. The silence after is the silence of the piece resolving — the king has spoken, the kingship is ended, what remains is accounting.

### Shot 5.51
**Visual:** Mac cecht. He is looking at the closed eyes. He does not move. His face holds.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** Silence. Wind through the broken Hostel.

**VO (Mac cecht, on-screen — his only line in the piece):**
> *Yes.*

**Source:** Authored. Mac cecht's single word of acknowledgment. The source has him weeping; we author a word.

**Production notes:** The single word is the acknowledgment. Mac cecht has carried the water across Ireland to be told he is a good man. That is the gift the king gives him. He receives it.

Alternative: cut the word entirely. Let the silence be his response. The shot then becomes 8 seconds of his face holding. This is a strong directorial choice and could be the right one. To be tested.

## Movement 6 — The cairn revisited, the accounting

Dawn. The piece resolves.

### Shot 5.52
**Visual:** Wide. Dawn over the plain. The Hostel — smoking ruin in the distance. Closer: the cairn (REF-AA). A figure is at it — taking a stone. Behind the figure, more figures approaching the cairn. The surviving reavers are reclaiming their stones.

**Duration:** 7s.

**Audio:** Wind. The sound of stones being taken from the pile — small dull thuds.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *Those who lived took back their stones.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 167.

## Production appendix

### Veo prompting principles for Act 5

- **The destruction restrained.** Throughout Act 5, the destruction is shown in middle distance and through specific named gestures (fires lit/extinguished, head crossings, cup, pour). It is not staged as battle. Veo: do not generate large-scale combat shots. Each violence-beat is a single composition.

- **The dry riverbed compositions (5.30–5.34).** The visual signature of the water-quest. Generate from a consistent prompt structure with varied topography. Same general composition — bank, dry channel, sky — with each river distinguishable. Achievable.

- **The Hostel burning (5.21, REF-W).** Middle distance. Smoke, flame visible through walls. Not full-frame fire. Veo handles middle-distance burning well; do not push closer.

- **The talking head (5.49).** **Highest-stakes shot of the piece.** Veo's earlier test was partial success. Production strategy: multiple takes, with fallback options sequenced from best to acceptable. Primary: head with subtle lip motion and opening eyes. Fallback 1: close crop hiding severance. Fallback 2: VO over Mac cecht's face. Lock the production approach early based on what Veo can deliver.

- **Lomna's severed head (5.10, REF-X).** Achievable per the seed's earlier Veo test on severed-head content. The mid-flight composition is the load-bearing image. Generate as a held composition with motion-blur applied.

- **The Hostel interior aftermath (5.40).** A wide of bodies in smoke. Content moderation risk: moderate. Strategy: smoky atmosphere obscures detail, bodies as indistinct shapes, Conaire's headless body as the only specifically resolved figure. If Veo refuses, fall back to a smoke-filled wide with only Conaire's body visible and the rest implied through silhouette.

- **The cairn (REF-AA).** Variation on Act 4's REF-U. Same composition, different state (lower, asymmetrical, stones removed). Achievable as a still with subtle Veo overlay (wind on grass, dawn light shifting).

### Voice notes

- **Narrator.** Returns to prominence. The voice is the same as Acts 1–4, but the register has shifted — quieter, slower, more elegiac. He is not explaining; he is counting. The diction shortens as the act progresses — by the head-crossings, he is using single words ("Fifth." "Sixth."). The accounting accelerates the closer it comes to numbers.

- **Mac cecht.** Single word, possibly. "Yes." If retained, this is the only word spoken by any character on Conaire's side in the entire piece. ElevenLabs casting: an exhausted voice. The voice of a man at the end of a long run. The performance question is whether "Yes" lands as acknowledgment or feels redundant against the head's speech. To be tested.

- **The talking head.** Two lines, verbatim Stokes. The voice must be small. Not theatrical. Not echoing. This is the king reduced to the minimum. The dignity is in the smallness. ElevenLabs casting: a voice that sounds like a young man rather than a king. The kingship is what was lost; what remains is the person.

### Suno / music notes

- **Almost entirely silent.** Act 5 strips out the audio bed of prior acts. Wind is the constant; specific sounds emerge against silence.
- **One Suno track:** the water-quest music (Movements 4). Sparse, breath sounds, low strings. Plays once across the river catalog. Resolves on Uaran Garad and ends. Direction: *"running breath, low strings, no rhythm, no melody — the sound of a man crossing distance."*
- **The held tone returns** at Shot 5.58. Same tone as Act 1. Very quiet. The closing structural rhyme.
- **The bass tone under the talking head (5.47–5.49).** New audio element. Opposite end of the supernatural marker register (high tone = Cailb/Badbs; bass tone = Conaire's head). One-time use.

### Edit notes

- The six crossings of Lomna's head are spread across Shots 5.10, 5.12, 5.14, 5.16, 5.18, 5.20. They are interleaved with the three fire sequences (5.13, 5.17, 5.19 and the extinguishings). Sequencing in the cut is critical — the two rhythms (head, fires) braid against each other and the narrator's diction tracks both.

- The transition from Movement 4 to Movement 5 is the first sound of water in the act. Mark this in the audio mix — it is structurally important.

- The held tone in Shot 5.58 should be subtle enough that some viewers may not consciously notice it. The structural rhyme is the work; conscious recognition is a bonus.

- Final cut to black holds 4 seconds, then silence. There should be no credits-track or post-roll audio bleed into the moments after the held tone resolves. The silence is the ending.
