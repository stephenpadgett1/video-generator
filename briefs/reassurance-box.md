# The Reassurance Box

A faux-DTC ad for a small wooden box that, when opened, says *"You made the right choice"* in a calm voice. About anything.

**Format:** ~30s, 9:16 vertical. Speedrun (sub-1hr).

---

## Tone

Glossy, sincere, lifestyle-DTC. Played completely straight — Calm app energy, sage-and-cream palette, soft natural light. The gag is the tension between the polished ad language and the absurd uselessness of the product. No winks.

---

## The product

A small wooden box. Plain. Walnut or oak. Brass hinge, no latch. A single tiny dot engraved center-lid (or nothing — ambiguity is the joke). Roughly palm-sized.

When opened, a calm warm voice says one line: **"You made the right choice."** Always the same line. About anything.

## Shot list

### 1. HERO — Product on plinth
Box on a small light-wood plinth, soft window light, sage wall behind. Static or barely-perceptible push-in. No hands. No copy. Presented like jewelry.

**Audio:** Soft sustained piano/pad. Room tone.

### 2. MAIN DEMO — The doubt → the box
Woman, 30s, at her kitchen counter, mid-morning. Holding her phone. A flicker of doubt crosses her face. She sets the phone down, picks up the box, opens it. *This is the only beat that teaches the audience the gag.*

**Audio:** Box opens with a soft wooden *tock*. Calm warm voice from the box: *"You made the right choice."* Her shoulders drop. Tiny smile.

### 3–6. CYCLE OF FOUR — Hands only
Identical mechanic in each: hands enter frame, open the box, the same recorded voice line plays. Each setting telegraphs a different doubt instantly. Anonymous everyperson hands — no character continuity needed.

**Identical voice line in all four** — drop the same audio file into post for each. Mechanical sameness IS the gag.

| Beat | Setting | What we read instantly |
|------|---------|------------------------|
| 3 | Tattoo chair, fresh ink visible on forearm | "I just got tatted." |
| 4 | Wedding dress in a church bathroom mirror | "I'm getting married right now." |
| 5 | Parked car at night, suitcase on passenger seat | "I'm leaving." |
| 6 | Hospital waiting room, papers on lap | Ambiguous-dark. Could be diagnosis, custody, signing-off. |

Escalation: petty → consequential → ruinous → ambiguous-dark. After beat 6, the air leaves the room. Testimonial reanimates it.

### 7. TESTIMONIAL — Face to camera
Woman, 30s, soft sweater, slightly out-of-focus living room background. Direct address. Warm half-smile. Slightly *off* — too placid, eyes a hair too steady.

**Line:** *"I left him at the altar this morning. I made the right choice."*

Mirrors cycle reveal #4 (wedding dress) — viewer connects them mid-line.

### 8. END CARD — Box + tagline
Cut back to the hero plate. Sans-serif type fades in beside the box.

```
THE REASSURANCE BOX
You made the right choice.
```

Beat. Cut to black.

## Audio

- **Box voice line** — ElevenLabs, warmest calm voice. Single line: *"You made the right choice."* Generate ONCE, reuse identical audio file for shots 2–6. Mood: peaceful. Tension: 0.1. Energy: 0.2. Meditation-app warm, not HAL.
- **Box-open foley** — soft wooden *tock*. Same SFX in every reveal (or near-identical).
- **Testimonial** — different voice, natural conversational, single take.
- **Music bed** — single sustained piano note or soft pad under hero, main demo, end card. Drop out under cycle reveals so the voice line lands clean each time. Optional swell back in for testimonial.
- **No VO narrator.** The product speaks. The testimonial speaks. That's it.

---

## Production notes

- **Hero box** generated once via nano-banana, reused as reference for every shot that contains it.
- **Cycle reveals** can be generated with the box reference + setting reference. Each is independent — no character lock, no book-end.
- **End card reuses the hero clip** with type overlay added in post. Not a separate generation.
- **Skip Fast drafts** — straight to Quality.
- **9:16 throughout.** Hero/end card framing: box centered, generous negative space for type.

**Generation budget:** 6 Veo Quality clips (hero, main demo, 4× cycle reveals, testimonial — wait, that's 7). Reusing hero for end card. 7 Veo gens + nano-banana refs. Tight at 1hr; will batch where possible.

---

## What success looks like

The viewer watches the whole thing thinking it's a real product. The escalation lands. The testimonial mirrors cycle reveal #4 and they catch it on the rewatch. The end card lands and they realize they'd actually buy one.
