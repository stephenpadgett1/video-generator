# Da Derga's Hostel — Act 4 Shooting Script v1
## "The Reavers' Counsel"

---

## Act-level notes

**Estimated runtime:** 5:30 – 7:00. This is the longest act. The chamber catalog cannot be rushed; the music of the piece is in the catalog's rhythm.

**Aspect ratio:** 16:9 throughout. No vertical breaks proposed.

**Voices in this act:**
- **Narrator** — minimal. The reavers do most of the talking.
- **Ingcél** — revealed in this act as the speaker of Thread 1. Five Thread 1 placements, one per full chamber. **Voice transition planned:** the archaic-prophetic register of Acts 2–3 should shift slightly toward embodied speech — same vocabulary, same formal cadence, but a man's voice with a man's breath. Subtle. The audience may or may not consciously register the shift; what they *should* register is that the line they've heard five times before is now coming from a mouth.
- **Fer rogain** — Ingcél's foster-brother, identifying the people of the Hostel. Plain register, grieving but practical. The catalog's "naming" voice.
- **Lomna Drúth** — the lamenter. His grief builds across the catalog and peaks at the Cormac Condlongas chamber (his recognition formula). Distinct voice — older, breaking, the only one in the piece we hear *grieving* aloud.
- **Conaire silent** throughout, as established.

**Music:**
- The pulse from Acts 2–3 is *gone* in this act. Replaced by an entirely new audio bed: low water (the sea behind the reavers), wind, and the sound of many bodies at rest — the host gathered offstage, audible but not visualized.
- Under each chamber description: when Ingcél describes a chamber, we hear *inside* — fragments of fire and voices from the chamber being scouted. The interior bleeds into the exterior through the cracks Ingcél is looking through.
- Suno: a single new instrumental piece, sparse, low strings and breath sounds, played beneath the catalog at very low volume. Builds across the act; reaches its peak under Conaire's chamber description; falls silent at the cairn.
- The Badbs chamber (Movement 3, chamber 4) has its own audio signature — a high single tone returns (the same one from under Cailb's litany). The structural rhyme: the supernatural marker.

**Reference images required:**
- **REF-Q:** Ingcél. A British exile, broad-shouldered, one-eyed (his missing eye marks him as Ingcél Cáech). Older than the foster-brothers. A face that has been planning this for years. Established in this act, will recur in Act 5.
- **REF-R:** Fer rogain. A man in his prime, a son of Donn Désa, foster-brother to Conaire. He looks like a man who loves the king he is about to kill.
- **REF-S:** Lomna Drúth. The fool. Older, bearded, weeping or close to it through the act. His face does the grief.
- **REF-T:** The reaver host gathered on the shore. A field of dark figures, indistinct individuals. Boats beached behind them. The sea further behind. Night.
- **REF-U:** The cairn. A pile of stones, knee-high, growing one stone at a time. Built on the plain near the Hostel.
- **REF-V:** The three Badbs in the rafters of the Hostel. Three pale figures suspended from the rooftree, ropes of slaughter at their necks, dark fluid pooling on the floor beneath them. The visual peak of the catalog. **Highest-risk content-moderation shot of the piece so far.**
- REF-A (Conaire), REF-H (Lé fri flaith), REF-P (Mac cecht) — established.

**Continuity from Act 3:**
- Act 3 ended on the exterior view of the Hostel, doors closed, light spilling from the seven doorways. Sounds rising from somewhere offstage — voices, water, the grind of boats.
- Act 4 opens on Ingcél's face. The voice the audience has been hearing.

## Movement 1 — The shore

The reavers established. Ingcél introduced visually but not yet as the voice.

### Shot 4.1
**Visual:** Black. Hold for a beat. Then fade up on a face — Ingcél (REF-Q). Close. Lit by a low fire offscreen. One eye visible; the other is in shadow at first, then resolves as missing — a scar, an empty socket. He is looking out at something offscreen. He is not speaking yet.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** The exterior sounds from end of Act 3 are now in foreground — sea, wind, the murmur of many men. A fire crackles close.

**VO:** None. The face holds in silence.

**Production notes:** This is the reveal *visually* before the audio reveal. The audience sees a one-eyed man whose face they have never seen. They do not yet know it is the voice. The voice will be revealed by the first Thread 1 placement in this act — but visually, we begin with the body.

Veo: hold this face. Minimal motion — slow blink, breath visible in cold air. The missing eye is structurally important and must be visible. If Veo gives us a perfectly intact face, regenerate.

### Shot 4.2
**Visual:** Pull back. Ingcél is sitting at a small fire on the shore. Around him, the host (REF-T) — hundreds of dark figures, indistinct, settled at rest. Boats beached behind. The sea beyond. The Hostel is visible in the middle distance, lit, on the plain.

**Duration:** 7s.

**Audio:** Same bed — sea, wind, low murmur of men, fire.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *On the shore of Howth, ten boats had been beached. From Britain they had come, with their leader Ingcél the One-Eyed, son of a king and exile from his land, and with him the foster-brothers of Conaire — Fer rogain and Lomna, sons of Donn Désa, exiled by the king they loved.*

**Source:** Stokes pp. 32, 64, compressed. The host is from Britain; Ingcél's exile is parallel to the foster-brothers'; the alliance is a covenant of reaving. The phrase "exiled by the king they loved" is authored — Stokes does not state it this baldly, but it is the structure.

**Production notes:** REF-T. The host is large but indistinct. Individual figures are not legible; the host is one mass. Veo: prompt for a field of seated dark figures, irregular spacing, fires interspersed. Do not let it default to a marching army.

### Shot 4.3
**Visual:** Two more figures at Ingcél's fire — **Fer rogain** (REF-R) and **Lomna Drúth** (REF-S). Fer rogain sits upright; Lomna is hunched, weeping silently. Neither has spoken yet.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Bed continues.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *They had built a cairn of stones earlier in the day — one stone for each man. The cairn stood on the plain, between the host and the Hostel.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 65, paraphrased. Stokes places the cairn-building earlier in the narrative; we reference it here without showing it (it will be shown directly in Movement 4 below).

**Production notes:** The cairn is named here but not seen. This is preparation — when we see it built in Movement 4, the audience already knows what it is for.

## Movement 2 — The scout

Ingcél goes alone to the Hostel. Returns. The catalog begins.

### Shot 4.4
**Visual:** Wide. The plain between the shore and the Hostel. A single figure — Ingcél — walking alone toward the Hostel. The host is behind him, on the shore. He walks slowly. He is not in a hurry.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** Wind. His footsteps. The interior sounds of the Hostel rise as he approaches.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *Ingcél went alone to scout the Hostel. They had agreed: he would look in, and his foster-brothers would name what he saw.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 65, paraphrased. The scouting structure is the source's — Ingcél has the British outsider's eye; Fer rogain and Lomna have the Irish foster-brothers' knowledge.

### Shot 4.5
**Visual:** Ingcél at the wall of the Hostel. He puts his one eye to a crack between boards. We see his face from outside — the eye against the light. The light through the crack stripes his cheek.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** The interior — fire, voices — louder now, more specific. He is listening.

**Production notes:** This composition will recur — Ingcél at a different crack for each chamber description. The visual rhythm of the catalog includes returning to this image, sometimes briefly, sometimes longer. Veo: light through a board-crack onto a face. Achievable, but watch for over-stylization (don't let it become noir).

### Shot 4.6
**Visual:** Cut to inside. Ingcél's POV through the crack — a slice of the Hostel interior. We see a small portion of a chamber: figures, fire, weapons. This is the first chamber. The view is narrow — bound by the wood at the edges of the crack.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Interior fully — voices, fire, the room.

**Production notes:** The crack-view composition is repeatable. Each chamber gets its own crack-view, distinct in lighting and what it reveals. Veo: prompt for a vertical letterbox of interior space, dark borders left and right (the wood of the wall). This becomes the visual signature of the catalog.

### Shot 4.7
**Visual:** Ingcél returns to the fire on the shore. Sits. Looks at Fer rogain. Begins to speak.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Bed returns — sea, wind, fire. The interior sound recedes.

## Movement 3 — The catalog

Five full chambers, each in the source rhythm: Ingcél describes → Fer rogain identifies → Lomna laments → Ingcél says "Clouds of weakness." With flash compressions between full chambers to give the source's cataloging rhythm without bloat.

### CHAMBER 1 — Cormac Condlongas

Site of Lomna's recognition formula (Thread 2 placement #3) and Thread 1 placement #1 of Act 4 — the reveal moment.

#### Shot 4.8
**Visual:** Ingcél at the fire, facing his foster-brothers. He begins his description. He is calm. His voice is the voice the audience has been hearing in Acts 2–3 — but now there is a face attached.

**Duration:** 14s.

**Audio:** Bed. Underneath, faintly, the interior sounds from the chamber being described.

**VO (Ingcél, on-screen):**
> *I saw a chamber, and in it nine men — beautiful, fair-haired, with brooches of gold. Among them, one who stood out — a man of mild speech and grave bearing, with grey at his temples though he was young. Beside him a hound of great size.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 66, compressed and trimmed. Ingcél's catalog descriptions in Stokes are longer and more ornamented; we compress to load-bearing imagery while preserving the formal-detailed register.

**Production notes:** This is Ingcél's first major speech. The voice shift from his Acts 2–3 register should be slight — same archaic vocabulary, same precision, but more breath. The audience may not consciously notice. They should, by the end of the speech, sense that this voice has a body now.

#### Shot 4.9
**Visual:** Crack-view interior. We see what Ingcél described — a group of men at hearth, a tall pale-haired warrior at center, a grey-streaked dog by his side. The view is restricted by the crack.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Interior closer. The men's quiet voices, the fire.

#### Shot 4.10
**Visual:** Fer rogain at the fire. Hand to his face. He has recognized the description. He speaks.

**Duration:** 8s.

**Audio:** Bed. Interior recedes.

**VO (Fer rogain, on-screen, plain register):**
> *That is Cormac Condlongas, son of Conchobar. The grey at his temples is his wisdom. The hound at his side is one of Conchobar's hounds. He is a good man, and his nine companions are the best of his people.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 66, compressed. Fer rogain identifies; the source's identifications include longer genealogies which we compress.

#### Shot 4.11 *(Thread 2 placement #3 — Lomna's recognition formula, the lament)*
**Visual:** Lomna. Close. His grief at full pitch now. He is the only one of the three who is broken open. Fer rogain holds together; Ingcél is calm; Lomna *grieves*.

**Duration:** 12s.

**Audio:** Bed drops back. Lomna's voice carries the silence.

**VO (Lomna Drúth, on-screen, archaic and broken):**
> *Woe is me before every one!*
>
> *Woe is me after every one!*
>
> *'Tis my head that will be first tossed about there to-night after an hour among the chariot-shafts.*

**Source:** Stokes pp. 67–68, verbatim. Thread 2 placement #3 — recognition as lament. Lomna foresees his own beheading. He is grieving his own death as much as he is grieving the people of the Hostel.

**Production notes:** Lomna's voice for the first time. The performance must be lament, not theatrics. Quiet, breaking, the voice of a man who knows. ElevenLabs: search for a voice capable of restrained grief — not weeping, but grief that has *settled*. The triple "woe" should land with three distinct beats; the second "every one" slightly louder than the first; the third line in a near-whisper.

The thematic weight of this placement: Lomna's recognition formula completes Thread 2. Welcome (Fer Caille), judgment (Cailb), lament (Lomna). The doom now mourns its own coming death.

#### Shot 4.12 *(Thread 1 placement #1 of Act 4 — THE REVEAL)*
**Visual:** Ingcél. His face. He is looking at Lomna. There is no contempt in his face — but there is no agreement either. He says the line.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** Silence under his voice. Then, after the line, the bed returns at low volume.

**VO (Ingcél, on-screen):**
> *Clouds of weakness are coming to you.*

**Source:** Stokes, verbatim. Ingcél's refrain to his foster-brothers.

**Production notes:** **This is the reveal.** The audience has heard this line five times across Acts 2–3 in a disembodied voice. Now they see who has been saying it. The line is the same. The register is — by design — only slightly different. The shock of the reveal is that it is *not* a different line. The doom they have been hearing has always been this man speaking to his own kin.

The casting choice for Ingcél across Acts 2–3 has been the same voice that delivers this line. The audience does not know that until this moment. The line, delivered with a body, recontextualizes every prior placement.

Hold on Ingcél's face for one beat after the line. He looks back to the Hostel.

### FLASH COMPRESSION — Chambers 2–4

Rapid catalog of three minor chambers, no full quartet. The rhythm we have established is briefly broken into accelerated fragments.

#### Shot 4.13
**Visual:** Rapid montage — three brief crack-views, each 2 seconds:

(a) **Conall Cernach's chamber.** A great red-haired warrior, his red shield. Three nines of men around him.

(b) **The three Manx giants.** Three enormous figures seated apart, their height visible even seated. Iron-grey hair.

(c) **The three Fomorian hostages.** Three figures of unusual proportion — too tall, too thin, eyes too far apart. Bound at the wrist with golden chains.

**Duration:** 6s total.

**Audio:** Each crack-view briefly opens its interior sound, then cuts to the next. Like a radio tuning through stations.

**Production notes:** The flash compressions are the source's cataloging *rhythm* without committing the full quartet to each name. Three two-second inserts. Veo: each is a discrete generation. The visual signature is consistent (crack-view, vertical letterbox of interior, dark borders).

#### Shot 4.14
**Visual:** Cut back to Ingcél at the fire. He has been speaking through the montage — we hear his voice over the flash images, listing names rapidly.

**Duration:** 8s.

**Audio:** His voice closes the flash compression.

**VO (Ingcél, over the flash montage and continuing into this shot):**
> *I saw red Conall, and nine times nine men. I saw three giants of Man. I saw three of the Fomorian race, in chains.*
>
> *Of these, Fer rogain spoke. Of these, my brother named the names.*

**Source:** Stokes pp. 71–75, radically compressed. The source spends pages on each of these; we compress to three names and a structural acknowledgment that the catalog continues.

**Production notes:** The line "Of these, Fer rogain spoke" — Ingcél acknowledging the catalog continues offscreen. We do not hear Fer rogain's identifications or Lomna's laments for the flash chambers. The structure is implied. This allows us to keep moving without losing the cataloging rhythm entirely.

### CHAMBER 2 — Lé fri flaith

Return to the full quartet. The boy from Act 2.

#### Shot 4.15
**Visual:** Ingcél, returning to scout. He moves to a different crack. His eye against the new opening.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** Bed. Interior shifts.

#### Shot 4.16
**Visual:** Crack-view. Inside the Hostel: a boy, seated near a man of kingly bearing. The boy is **Lé fri flaith** (REF-H). He is not eating. His face is the face we saw at the end of Act 2 — a child who has heard a doom-poem and carried it.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Interior — the gathering's voices, but the boy is silent.

#### Shot 4.17
**Visual:** Ingcél, back at the fire.

**Duration:** 10s.

**VO (Ingcél, on-screen):**
> *I saw a child. Hair of three colors — gold, red, and dark. He was not eating. He sat by his father, and his father did not speak. The child looked at the door as though the door were already broken.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 77, compressed. The three-colored hair is preserved from Act 2's reference. The detail about the door is authored — the source has Lé fri flaith weeping; the door-watching is our addition. **Flag: this is invention.** Defensible because it connects Act 2 (the doom-poem) to Act 4 (the doom's arrival) through the boy.

#### Shot 4.18
**Visual:** Fer rogain. He cannot look at Ingcél now. He speaks to the fire.

**Duration:** 7s.

**VO (Fer rogain, on-screen):**
> *That is Lé fri flaith, son of Conaire. He is seven years old. He carries a doom-poem inside him that he has not spoken aloud since he heard it.*

**Source:** Stokes — Lé fri flaith's identification, with the doom-poem reference authored to connect to Act 2. The age is from Stokes (Lé fri flaith is consistently described as young).

#### Shot 4.19
**Visual:** Lomna. He says only one line. He cannot say more.

**Duration:** 5s.

**VO (Lomna Drúth, on-screen):**
> *Woe to him who shall wreak the Destruction. He is a child.*

**Source:** Stokes — "woe to him who shall wreak the Destruction" is Lomna's refrain in the source; "he is a child" is authored.

#### Shot 4.20 *(Thread 1 placement #2 of Act 4)*
**Visual:** Ingcél. His eyes do not move. His mouth opens.

**Duration:** 4s.

**VO (Ingcél, on-screen):**
> *Clouds of weakness are coming to you.*

### CHAMBER 3 — Mac cecht

The champion. Sets up Act 5.

#### Shot 4.21
**Visual:** Crack-view. Mac cecht (REF-P) at his place. The spear leaning. By the wall: the weapons that fell in Act 3's spark sequence are visible, still on the floor. He has not moved them.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** Interior — quieter than the previous chambers. Mac cecht is alone in his pool of light.

#### Shot 4.22
**Visual:** Ingcél.

**Duration:** 10s.

**VO (Ingcél, on-screen):**
> *I saw a man of great size. A spear stood against the wall by his hand. At his feet were weapons that had fallen — many of them — and he had not picked them up. He was waiting. I do not know for what.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 79, compressed. The fallen weapons connect directly to Act 3's spark sequence — Ingcél is seeing them from outside, having heard their fall earlier. He does not know what they mean. The audience does.

**Production notes:** Ingcél's confusion ("I do not know for what") is one of the few moments of uncertainty in his voice across the act. Worth playing — he does not understand everything he sees. The fallen weapons are a portent he cannot read.

#### Shot 4.23
**Visual:** Fer rogain.

**Duration:** 8s.

**VO (Fer rogain, on-screen):**
> *That is Mac cecht, Conaire's champion. The weapons that fell are no man's doing. They fall when the king is in danger. Mac cecht has not moved them because he is reading them.*

**Source:** Stokes — Mac cecht as Conaire's champion is canonical. The interpretation of the fallen weapons is authored — Stokes describes the portent without explaining who reads it. We give the reading to Mac cecht. This sets up his role in Act 5.

#### Shot 4.24
**Visual:** Lomna.

**Duration:** 4s.

**VO (Lomna Drúth, on-screen):**
> *Woe to him who shall meet Mac cecht in arms.*

**Source:** Stokes, Lomna's refrain pattern.

#### Shot 4.25 *(Thread 1 placement #3 of Act 4)*
**Visual:** Ingcél.

**Duration:** 4s.

**VO (Ingcél, on-screen):**
> *Clouds of weakness are coming to you.*

### CHAMBER 4 — The Badbs

The visual peak. Supernatural marker. The high single tone returns.

#### Shot 4.26
**Visual:** Ingcél at a crack. But this time, his face changes as he looks. The calm slips for a moment. Then returns.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Bed. The high sustained tone from Cailb's litany (Act 3.13) enters underneath. A structural rhyme — same tone, same supernatural register.

#### Shot 4.27
**Visual:** **REF-V. Crack-view.** Three pale figures suspended from the rooftree of the Hostel. Bound at the neck with dark cords. Beneath them, on the floor, dark fluid pools. The figures' faces are turned away or hidden in shadow. Nobody in the Hostel below is looking at them — the warriors continue their gathering, oblivious.

**Duration:** 7s.

**Audio:** Interior, with the high tone overlay. The voices in the Hostel are unchanged — they do not see what we see.

**Source:** Stokes pp. 75–76, paraphrased. The Badbs (war-hags) appear in the rafters as portents during the cataloging in the source. Three of them, with ropes of slaughter, three streams of blood. They are visible only to those who can see — Ingcél sees; the people of the Hostel do not.

**Production notes:** **Highest-risk content-moderation shot of the piece.** Strategy: composition that strongly suggests without showing. The Badbs are pale figures in the upper rafters — lit only from below by the hearth — faces in shadow or turned away. The blood-streams are implied: darkness on the floor below them, catching torchlight as wet. Nudity is not the issue (the figures can be cloaked); the issue is the suspended-bodies imagery, which Veo may refuse.

Fallback if Veo refuses: render the Badbs as silhouettes against the high rafters, with no visible suspension mechanism — they could be standing on a beam, or they could be hanging; the ambiguity is the point. Or: render only the *consequence* — the dark pool on the floor, with three indistinct shapes high above just out of focus.

If absolutely refused: the chamber can be carried entirely on Ingcél's description and Lomna's reaction. The image would be a loss but not a structural failure.

#### Shot 4.28
**Visual:** Ingcél, back at the fire. His calm has fully returned. But something has registered.

**Duration:** 12s.

**VO (Ingcél, on-screen):**
> *I saw three figures in the rafters of the Hostel. They were not living. They were not yet dead. They were bound at the neck with cords I have not seen before, and below them the floor was dark, and the people of the Hostel did not see them.*
>
> *I do not know what they are.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 76, compressed. Ingcél's uncertainty is honored — in the source he asks Fer rogain what they are.

#### Shot 4.29
**Visual:** Fer rogain. He has gone pale. He answers without looking up.

**Duration:** 8s.

**VO (Fer rogain, on-screen):**
> *Those are the Badbs. They come when a battle is to be fought. They are seen by some and not by others. They have been there a long time. Conaire could not see them. Conaire never could.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 76, compressed and elaborated. The Badbs are identified in the source; their invisibility to the doomed is authored from the structural implication.

**Production notes:** "Conaire never could" — a small, devastating line. It does work the source only implies.

#### Shot 4.30
**Visual:** Lomna. He has stopped weeping. He says the line flat.

**Duration:** 4s.

**VO (Lomna Drúth, on-screen):**
> *Woe to those whom the Badbs already see.*

**Source:** Stokes, refrain pattern, with content authored.

#### Shot 4.31 *(Thread 1 placement #4 of Act 4)*
**Visual:** Ingcél. The high tone is still underneath.

**Duration:** 5s.

**VO (Ingcél, on-screen):**
> *Clouds of weakness are coming to you.*

The high tone resolves after the line — falls away.

### FLASH COMPRESSION — Chambers 5–8

A second flash compression. The catalog continues in fragments. We are accelerating toward Conaire.

#### Shot 4.32
**Visual:** Rapid montage — four crack-views, 1.5 seconds each:

(a) **The three judges.** Three robed figures at a small table. Stillness.

(b) **The three poets.** Three men with harps. One is playing silently — fingers move on strings but no sound emerges (the audio bed has dropped).

(c) **The three lampooners.** Three men laughing. Mouths wide. No sound.

(d) **The three swineherds.** Three men by a hearth, a pig roasting between them.

**Duration:** 6s total.

**Audio:** The bed has dropped to near-silence. Each insert is silent. The visual is more eerie for the absence of sound.

**Production notes:** The silent inserts are the catalog accelerating *and* the world starting to lose its own sound. By the time we reach Conaire's chamber, the audio has been stripped almost bare. This is the bridge to the long pause that closes Movement 3.

#### Shot 4.33
**Visual:** Ingcél at the fire, listing names rapidly. The fire pops — the only sound.

**Duration:** 6s.

**VO (Ingcél, on-screen):**
> *I saw judges. I saw poets. I saw lampooners. I saw the three swineherds. I saw the three who tend the hearths and the three who carry water and the three who sing and the three who do not.*
>
> *Of these, Fer rogain spoke. Of these, my brother named the names.*

**Source:** Stokes — radical compression of the cataloging that occupies much of the central source. The litany of threes echoes the source's structural obsession with the number. The "Of these, Fer rogain spoke" line returns from Shot 4.14 — its repetition is structural.

### CHAMBER 5 — Conaire Himself

The final chamber. The longest. The reason for everything.

#### Shot 4.34
**Visual:** Ingcél at his crack. He has not moved to a new one. This is the central crack. The view is wider here — a longer slice of light.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** The Suno track that has been beneath the catalog reaches its peak — still very low, but more present. Strings. Breath.

#### Shot 4.35
**Visual:** Crack-view. **The chamber of the king.** Conaire (REF-A) at the central hearth. His son Lé fri flaith beside him. His retinue around him. The composition is the same as Shot 3.7 (the interior wide from Act 3), but viewed now through the crack — bound by the wood.

**Duration:** 8s.

**Audio:** Interior — the king's chamber sound bed, restored briefly. Fire, voices, the warmth.

**Production notes:** The audience has seen this chamber before — Movement 3 of Act 3, the establishing interior. Now they see it through Ingcél's eye, framed by the wood of the wall. The composition is the same; the meaning is opposite.

#### Shot 4.36
**Visual:** Ingcél at the fire. His longest single speech. The audience needs to feel the description has *weight* — this is the description of the perfect king. Ingcél does not understand what he is describing; he is reading a portrait without recognizing the model. Fer rogain and Lomna do.

**Duration:** 30s.

**Audio:** The Suno track holds. Bed is otherwise minimal.

**VO (Ingcél, on-screen):**
> *I saw a man at the central hearth. He was young — younger than I expected of a king. His face was without a flaw. His hair was the color of a fox's coat at autumn. He wore a mantle of deep red, and a brooch of gold at his shoulder.*
>
> *Beside him, a child with hair of three colors. Around him, his people. Behind him, his weapons untouched.*
>
> *He has not spoken since I began to watch. He has not eaten. He has not raised his voice. His hand rests on the child's shoulder and the child does not look up.*
>
> *I have never seen a man so still.*

**Source:** Stokes pp. 84–85, compressed and re-authored. Stokes's description of Conaire's chamber is one of the most beautiful passages in the saga. We compress to load-bearing imagery while preserving the formal-portrait register. The detail "He has not spoken since I began to watch" is authored — connecting Ingcél's observation to the audience's knowledge that Conaire has never spoken in the piece.

**Production notes:** This is Ingcél's longest speech and the verbal peak of the act. The pacing must hold. ElevenLabs delivery: slow, considered, building. The voice carries its own ignorance — Ingcél is describing a man he does not recognize. By the end of the speech, the audience should feel his quiet wonder; they should also feel the gulf between his wonder and what is about to happen.

#### Shot 4.37
**Visual:** Fer rogain. He cannot keep his composure for this one. His hand is over his mouth. He speaks through his fingers.

**Duration:** 14s.

**VO (Fer rogain, on-screen):**
> *That is the king.*
>
> *That is Conaire son of Eterscél. That is our foster-brother. That is the man whose hospitality we ate as children, the man whose mother fed us when our mothers were dead, the man whose laughter we knew before we knew our own names.*
>
> *Ingcél, that is the king.*

**Source:** Stokes — radically expanded from the source. Fer rogain's identification of Conaire is the most painful in the catalog; the source treats it with restraint. We give it more breath here because it carries the weight of the entire catalog's structural argument: the destroyers love what they destroy.

#### Shot 4.38 *(Lomna's grief at peak)*
**Visual:** Lomna. He is not weeping now. He has gone past weeping. His face is empty.

**Duration:** 10s.

**VO (Lomna Drúth, on-screen):**
> *I will not say woe. There is no woe great enough.*
>
> *I have already named my head. I have already named the destruction. There is nothing left in my mouth.*

## Production appendix

### Veo prompting principles for Act 4

- **The catalog's visual rhythm.** The crack-view composition (vertical letterbox bound by dark wood) is the visual signature of the act. Every chamber description has its own crack-view. Veo: generate a base composition for the crack — same width, same dark borders — and vary only the interior contents. This consistency is structural.

- **Ingcél's face across the act.** The single most-rendered face in this act, probably the piece. Lock the reference (REF-Q) hard before generating any Act 4 footage. The missing eye must be visible and consistent across every shot. Watch Veo for "healing" the eye between generations.

- **Fer rogain's emotional arc.** He starts composed and ends with hand to mouth. Veo: each chamber description shot of Fer rogain should show slightly more strain than the last. Subtle. Not theatrical. The audience reads the arc cumulatively.

- **Lomna's grief.** He weeps in the early shots. He goes empty by Conaire's chamber. The progression is the opposite of intensification — grief that outruns its own expression. Veo: prompt for the specific facial register at each placement, do not let it default to a generic "sad man."

- **The Badbs (REF-V, Shot 4.27).** Highest-risk content-moderation shot. Strategy laid out in shot notes. Test early — if Veo refuses across multiple framings, fall back to silhouette/implication. The shot can be lost without structural failure but is the visual peak of the catalog.

- **The cairn building (4.40).** Either a single time-lapse-style generation, or three to four sequential generations with the cairn growing. Achievable.

- **The host advancing (4.47).** Three hundred men walking at a measured pace. Veo will resist large crowd scenes; even when delivered, individual figures will be inconsistent. Strategy: keep the host at middle distance or further; do not ask Veo to render faces in the host. The host is *one mass* moving forward.

### Voice notes

- **Ingcél across the act.** Five long speeches plus five Thread 1 placements. This is the most extensive single role in the piece. The voice transition from Acts 2–3 must be subtle — same vocabulary, same precision, but a man's breath behind it. The audience should not consciously notice the shift until the reveal moment (4.12), at which point the line they have heard five times in disembodied form is suddenly attached to a face and a breath.

- **Fer rogain.** Steadily breaking. Five identifications across the act, plus the flash-compression line. The final identification (Conaire, 4.37) is his peak. ElevenLabs casting: a voice that can hold composure and then fail to hold it.

- **Lomna Drúth.** Five lament placements, each shorter than the last. The arc is *toward silence*. By Conaire's chamber (4.38), he has run out of refrain. Casting: a voice capable of grief without theatrics.

- **The narrator.** Minimal in this act. Bookends only — opening exposition (4.2, 4.3) and the cairn explanation (4.42). His voice should sound smaller than it did in Act 1, where he ruled the soundscape; here he is barely present.

### Suno / music notes

- **No pulse.** The pulse from Acts 2–3 does not return in Act 4. Replaced by a single instrumental piece — sparse, low strings and breath sounds — that plays beneath the entire catalog at very low volume. Suno prompt direction: *"low strings, breath sounds, no melody, no rhythm, building slowly across six minutes, peaks at minute five, ends in silence."*
- **The high tone returns** under the Badbs chamber (4.26–4.31). Same tone as Cailb's litany. Structural rhyme. Mark this in production.
- **Silence under the flash compressions** (4.13, 4.32) — the audio bed drops. The flash chambers are visually present and aurally absent. The strangeness of silent imagery is the point.
- **All audio strips** under Thread 1 placement #5 (4.39). Conaire's chamber description's Suno track ends; the bed strips out; only Ingcél's voice. The pause after carries 5 seconds of clean silence.

### Edit notes

- The crack-view composition is the structural signature. Eight crack-view shots across the act (Shots 4.6, 4.9, 4.13a-c, 4.16, 4.21, 4.27, 4.32a-d, 4.35). Each must read consistently as "through the wall" — same letterbox dimensions, same dark border treatment.
- The Suno track and the bed silences need careful sequencing. The track is *always at low volume*. Audibility is structural — its presence and absence must register.
- The closing cut to black holds three seconds before Act 5. The longest sustained black of the piece.

## Open items raised by drafting

- **The voice shift for Ingcél at the reveal.** I have written the shift as subtle — same vocabulary, same precision, slightly more embodied breath. The full plan was to allow the shift to "land where it lands." If in production the shift is so subtle as to be inaudible, that may be the right outcome (the reveal is purely visual). If it's too strong, the reveal collapses into a different voice altogether. The performance target is *the same line, terrifyingly ordinary*. To be tested.

- **Lé fri flaith's invented detail (4.17).** I authored the line about the boy "looking at the door as though the door were already broken." Defensible because it connects Acts 2 and 4 through the boy. But it is invention. If the source-integrity principle wants tightening, replace with Stokes-original detail (the source has Lé fri flaith weeping).

- **The Badbs chamber (4.26–4.31).** Highest content-moderation risk. Three fallback strategies sketched in shot notes. Need to test early. The chamber is the visual peak of the catalog and structurally important (the supernatural marker returning the high tone from Cailb's litany), but it can be lost without structural failure.

- **Lomna's emptiness at Conaire's chamber (4.38).** I authored a refusal of his refrain pattern — "I will not say woe. There is no woe great enough." This is a strong invention. Defensible: it is the structural moment where grief outruns language, and Lomna has been building toward this point. But it is invention. Alternative: keep him in refrain pattern ("Woe to him who shall wreak the Destruction of his foster-brother") and let the refrain itself fail through repetition.

- **The hand-raised "go" gesture (4.46).** Ingcél commits the host wordlessly. The source has Ingcél give an order; we have made it gestural. Defensible (consistent with the act's reduction-toward-silence) but a departure.

- **Fer rogain's final identification (4.37).** I expanded this from Stokes considerably. The "we ate his mother's bread" detail is the spirit of the source's relationships, but the specific imagery is authored. If kept, it is one of the most load-bearing authored moments of the piece. If cut to Stokes-original brevity, the catalog's emotional peak shifts to Lomna alone.

- **The host's final advance (4.47).** I have them walking, not running. The source is ambiguous but suggests something more aggressive. The walking is a directorial choice — the inevitability of the act in its slowness. If "running" feels truer to the source's energy, easily revised.

- **Whether Cailb appears anywhere in the catalog.** I have not included her — she is inside the Hostel by Act 4, having entered at end of Act 3, but Ingcél does not describe her. The source does not describe her among the chambers either; she is structurally separate from the catalog. I have honored this. But if her absence from the catalog feels like an omission, she could be inserted as a flash compression beat (single crack-view of the heron-posture figure at the edge of the gathering).
