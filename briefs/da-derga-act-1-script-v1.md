# Da Derga's Hostel — Act 1 Shooting Script v1
## "The Peace and the Binding"

---

## Act-level notes

**Estimated runtime:** 2:30 – 3:45.

**Aspect ratio:** 16:9 throughout. No vertical breaks proposed for Act 1.

**Voices in this act:**
- **Narrator** (modernized prose, archaic register when reciting verbatim Stokes peace markers). One voice.
- **Nemglan** (archaic, formal, binding). Distinct voice. ElevenLabs — character is partially obscured / transforming, so VO is appropriate. Decision on whether this voice is shared with the doom-voice in Acts 2–3 is **deferred** (current lean: distinct).
- **Conaire silent.** No dialogue. His only direct line is the talking-head poem in Act 5.

**Music:**
- **Peace (cold open):** no music. Ambient outdoor sound only — distant birdsong, faint water, a wind that moves nothing visible. Possibly a single sustained low tone, almost subliminal.
- **Bull-feast through coronation:** sparse score. Suno track tentatively titled "binding" — slow, formal, low strings or held drone with a single melodic figure. No percussion.
- **Peace (return):** music drops out. Same ambient bed as cold open.

**Reference images required (Nano Banana Pro):**
- **REF-A:** Conaire base character. Young, beardless, dark or auburn hair, slim. A face that reads as a king-in-waiting rather than a warrior. Will be reused throughout the piece and must be locked first.
- **REF-B:** Nemglan in bird-form. A large white bird, speckled, of unusual size — closer to a swan than a crow, but stranger. Standing on rock at the sea's edge.
- **REF-C:** Nemglan in man-form. Mid-transformation register — a man who has just left a birdskin behind. Birdskin visible at his feet. Sea behind him.
- **REF-D:** Bull-feast seer. An older man, bearded, supine on a hide, eyes closed. A slain bull nearby. Firelight.
- **REF-E:** The road of Tara at dawn. Empty, ceremonial, wide.
- **REF-F:** Tara — a low ceremonial mound at the edge of an open plain. Not a castle. A place rather than a building.
- Reference image **#3 (peace — cow in still meadow, vertical smoke, held dewdrops)** already established and approved.

**First/last frame strategy:**
- Peace cold open and peace return should share visual vocabulary but not be identical. The return is the same world, slightly differently held — closer to dusk than midday.
- Coronation final frame anchors directly into return-to-peace opening frame (color/light continuity).

## Movement 1 — The Peace (cold open)

Held images. No narration. The world is impossibly still.

### Shot 1.1
**Visual:** Wide landscape. A cow stands in a meadow at midday. Dewdrops on the grass — they have not fallen. Behind the cow, smoke from an unseen hearth rises in a perfectly straight vertical line into a cloudless sky. The cow's tail does not move.

**Duration:** 8s.

**Audio:** Ambient outdoor — a single distant bird. No wind sound. A held low tone, just at the threshold of audibility.

**Source:** Stokes pp. 28, 167–168 (peace markers). Reference image #3.

**Production notes:** This is the first image of the piece. Hold it long. Veo's tendency toward subtle motion (leaves, grass, cow tail) needs to be actively countered — the impossibility of stillness is the point. Prompt should explicitly request held stillness; if Veo cannot deliver, generate a still image and use it as a held frame with subtle ambient sound layered over. Reverse-loop trick acceptable here.

### Shot 1.2
**Visual:** Close. Dewdrops on a single blade of grass. The sun is high. They have not fallen.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Continuation of ambient bed.

**Source:** Stokes p. 167 — *"not a dewdrop fell from grass till midday."*

**Production notes:** Macro shot. Nano Banana reference image as bookend if Veo struggles with the held suspension.

### Shot 1.3
**Visual:** Wide. A river. Fish visible just under the surface, abundant. The water is glass-still.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** Faint water sound — too faint for a real river. The held tone continues.

**Source:** Stokes p. 28 — *"plenty of fish in the rivers Bush and Boyne."*

### Shot 1.4
**Visual:** A forest floor, knee-deep in fallen acorns and oakmast. Light filters down through bare branches. Nothing moves.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Continuation. The low tone deepens almost imperceptibly.

**Source:** Stokes p. 28 — *"oakmast up to the knees in every autumn."*

### Shot 1.5 *(transition shot)*
**Visual:** Return to the wide meadow of 1.1. Same composition, same cow. The light has shifted very slightly toward afternoon. Still nothing moves.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** Tone holds. The narrator's first breath audible just before he speaks.

**Production notes:** This is the bridge into Movement 2. Shot 1.5 last frame anchors to Shot 1.6 first frame.

## Movement 2 — The making of the king

Narrator opens. Brief lineage. Bull-feast compressed.

### Shot 1.6
**Visual:** Continuation of the meadow. As narrator speaks, the camera begins, very slowly, a push-in toward the smoke column — the only vertical line in the frame.

**Duration:** 10s.

**Audio:** Narrator enters. Suno "binding" track begins beneath, very low.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *There was a king in Erin once. His name was Conaire, son of Mess Buachalla. His mother was a foundling, raised in the calf-shed of cowherds. His father was a bird who came through a skylight at dawn and left his birdskin on the floor. The boy was raised in three households at once, and he grew with three gifts: hearing, eyesight, and judgment.*

**Source:** Stokes pp. 19–22 (compressed).

**Production notes:** Narrator's register is plain modern English here — connective tissue. Hold the visual still under the voice. The push-in should be barely perceptible.

### Shot 1.7
**Visual:** Hard cut. Firelight. A black bull lies dead on a stone floor. An older man sits beside it, drinking from a wooden bowl.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Suno track louder. A wet sound from the bowl.

**Source:** Stokes p. 22–23 — bull-feast.

**Production notes:** REF-D. Compressed bull-feast. We do not see the slaughter; we see the aftermath and the seer beginning to consume.

### Shot 1.8
**Visual:** The seer is now supine on a hide, eyes closed. The bowl is empty beside him. A figure (unseen, at the edge of frame, a cloak) stands over him chanting silently — lips moving, no sound.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Suno track. The held tone returns under the music — they layer.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *A bull was killed. A man ate his fill of it and drank its broth. A spell of truth was chanted over him in his sleep. Whoever he saw in his dream would be king of Erin.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 23.

### Shot 1.9
**Visual:** The seer's dream. A long road at dawn. A young man walks the road, naked, carrying a sling. We see him from behind, distant.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** Suno track. Wind for the first time.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *He saw a man, stark naked, walking the road of Tara at the end of night, with a stone in his sling.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 23. REF-A (Conaire) for the figure, framed from behind so face is not yet seen.

**Production notes:** This is the prophecy that will be fulfilled in Shot 1.21. Visual rhyme — keep composition similar.

## Movement 3 — The birds and Nemglan

Conaire pursues the speckled birds to the sea.

### Shot 1.10
**Visual:** Wide. A different landscape — the Plain of Liffey. A single chariot stands halted. Conaire (REF-A, full face visible for the first time) is turning his head, looking up.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Suno track shifts — a single melodic figure enters. Distant birdcall — speckled, unusual, not native.

**Source:** Stokes p. 23–24.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *But the king's son was not at Tara. He was at his games on the plain when great white-speckled birds came over him — birds of unusual size and color, beauty he had never seen. He left his fosterbrothers and turned his chariot toward the sea.*

### Shot 1.11
**Visual:** Chariot in motion. Coast in the distance. The birds — large, white, speckled — flying ahead, always one spear-cast distant.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Music carries. Hooves. Wind. The birds' wings audible.

**Production notes:** Veo can handle this. Keep the birds clearly visible — not a flock, a discrete number (six or seven), large enough to read as singular individuals. Watch for Veo defaulting to generic seabirds. Specify "large white speckled birds, larger than swans, of unusual size."

### Shot 1.12
**Visual:** Conaire on foot at the shore. Sling in hand, raised. The birds have settled on the wave just offshore.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Sea. Wind. Music thinning.

**Source:** Stokes p. 24.

### Shot 1.13
**Visual:** The birds on the wave. Mid-frame, one of them begins to change. The birdskin opens. A man steps out of it onto the water — Nemglan (REF-C). The other birds, behind him, are also transforming, but we hold on him.

**Duration:** 7s.

**Audio:** Music drops to almost nothing. The held tone returns. A sound like a sail tearing — the moment of transformation.

**Source:** Stokes p. 24 — *"The birds quit their birdskins, and turn upon him with spears and swords. One of them protects him."*

**Production notes:** This is the visual challenge of Act 1. Veo may struggle with the bird-to-man transformation. Strategy: generate the transformation as a single shot if possible; if not, generate (a) bird on wave, (b) birdskin on water with man emerging, and (c) man on wave with birdskin behind him as separate clips, splice in edit. Nano Banana first/last frame anchors strongly recommended — REF-B as first frame, REF-C as last frame, let Veo interpolate.

### Shot 1.14
**Visual:** Nemglan, on the water at the shore's edge, addressing Conaire. Conaire stands at the water-line, sling lowered. Behind Nemglan, the other transformed birdmen are armed but motionless.

**Duration:** 8s.

**Audio:** Sea, wind. Held tone. No music.

**VO (Nemglan, on screen, ElevenLabs):**
> *I am Nemglan, king of thy father's birds. Thou hast been forbidden to cast at birds, for here there is no one that should not be dear to thee, because of his father or mother.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 24, verbatim.

**Production notes:** Veo dialogue handling. Nemglan's mouth is partially obscured by a slight backlight / mist from the sea — buys forgiveness if lip-sync is imperfect. ElevenLabs voice should be archaic, formal, neither warm nor cold. A binding voice.

## Movement 4 — The Geasa

Eight prohibitions, verbatim Stokes. The formal heart of the act.

**Block strategy:** held single shot of Nemglan, full frame, sea behind him. The prohibitions land in a slow, formal cadence with a clear beat between each. No cuts. No visual variation. The unbroken visual is the binding made manifest.

### Shot 1.15
**Visual:** Nemglan, three-quarter frame, sea behind. He is still. The light does not change. The wind does not move his hair.

**Duration:** 40s.

**Audio:** A held low tone returns. No music. The sea is quieter than it should be.

**VO (Nemglan, ElevenLabs):**
> *Thy reign will be subject to a restriction. This shall be thy tabu.*
>
> *Thou shalt not go righthandwise round Tara and lefthandwise round Bregia.*
>
> *The evil-beasts of Cerna must not be hunted by thee.*
>
> *Thou shalt not go out every ninth night beyond Tara.*
>
> *Thou shalt not sleep in a house from which firelight is manifest outside, after sunset, and in which light is manifest from without.*
>
> *Three Reds shall not go before thee to Red's house.*
>
> *No rapine shall be wrought in thy reign.*
>
> *After sunset, a company of one woman or one man shall not enter the house in which thou art.*
>
> *And thou shalt not settle the quarrel of thy two thralls.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 27, verbatim.

**Production notes:** This is the longest single shot of Act 1 and possibly the piece. It must hold. Veo cannot generate 40 seconds in a single clip — strategy is to generate a held base shot (8s) with Nemglan present and minimal motion, and loop/extend in edit using crossfades or held still frames. The voice carries the time; the visual carries weight by not changing. ElevenLabs delivery is critical — slow, formal, eight prohibitions with clear structural beats. Aim for ~4 seconds per prohibition with ~1s pause between. Total VO ~38–42s.

Watch Veo for unwanted ambient motion (waves, hair, birds in background). The unnatural stillness of this shot is its load.

## Movement 5 — Coronation

Compressed. Wordless.

### Shot 1.16
**Visual:** The road of Tara at dawn. Conaire walks the road, naked (modesty handled by composition — distant, from behind, low light), sling in hand. Three figures wait at the edge of the road, holding folded raiment.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Suno "binding" track returns, fuller now.

**Source:** Stokes p. 25 — fulfillment of the bull-feast prophecy.

**Production notes:** Visual rhyme to Shot 1.9 (the seer's dream). Same road, same composition, same figure from behind. The dream made flesh.

REF-A (Conaire) and REF-E (road of Tara). For modesty / Veo content moderation: low light, distance, composition emphasizing the act of walking rather than the body.

### Shot 1.17
**Visual:** Closer. The three kings (figures) place raiment on Conaire. He does not speak. They acclaim him in silence — mouths open in shout, no sound emerges.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** Suno track. The shout is silent — a deliberate withholding.

**Source:** Stokes pp. 25–26.

**Production notes:** The silent shout is a directorial choice — Conaire's reign begins in silence, a silence that will hold until his severed head speaks in Act 5. Veo will resist generating silent shouting; prompt explicitly for "open mouths, no sound, ceremonial gesture." Audio is muted in edit regardless of what Veo generates.

### Shot 1.18
**Visual:** Wide. Tara. Conaire at the center of a small assembly. Robed. Crowned (a simple circlet, not gold-heavy). The figures around him recede into the background.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Suno track resolves on a held chord. The held tone returns underneath.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *They set the kingship of Erin upon him.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 26, verbatim.

## Movement 6 — The peace (return)

Same vocabulary, slightly different light. The world we were shown at the start, now understood as held in place by what we have just witnessed.

### Shot 1.19
**Visual:** The wide meadow of Shot 1.1. Same cow. Same vertical smoke. Light is later — long afternoon shadows, but everything still. The dewdrops are gone now (it is past midday, and we know this matters).

**Duration:** 7s.

**Audio:** Music falls away. Ambient bed returns. The held tone is still there, slightly higher in pitch.

**VO (Narrator, register shifted toward archaic — the peace markers verbatim):**
> *In his reign, no cloud veiled the sun for the space of a day from the middle of spring to the middle of autumn. Wind would not touch a beast's tail until nones. From year's end to year's end no wolf attacked aught save one bull-calf of each byre. Each man's voice seemed to his fellow as sweet as the strings of lutes.*

**Source:** Stokes pp. 28, 167–168 (peace markers). Lightly trimmed for rhythm per the seed's text-handling rules.

**Production notes:** Narrator shifts register here — same voice, archaic vocabulary, slower cadence. This is the only place in Act 1 where the narrator delivers verbatim Stokes. Marks the transition from "explanation" to "what was."

### Shot 1.20
**Visual:** Three crowns laid on a stone. Crown of corn-ears. Crown of flowers. Crown of oak mast. They are simple, woven things — not metalwork.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Ambient bed. Held tone.

**VO (Narrator, continuing):**
> *On Erin were three crowns: a crown of corn-ears, a crown of flowers, a crown of oak mast.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 168, verbatim.

**Production notes:** Single still composition. Could be a Nano Banana still rather than a Veo clip — no motion required.

### Shot 1.21
**Visual:** Return to the wide meadow once more. The light is approaching dusk now. The cow is still there. The smoke still rises straight. But the shadow it casts is longer.

**Duration:** 8s.

**Audio:** Held tone alone. The bird-call from Shot 1.1 does not return.

**VO:** None.

**Production notes:** Last frame of Act 1. The held tone carries through black to the first frame of Act 2.

## Production appendix

### Veo prompting principles for Act 1

- **Stillness is the recurring problem.** Veo defaults to subtle motion. Every peace shot needs an explicit "no movement, no wind, no swaying" instruction, and even then will likely require either still-image generation or reverse-loop editing for the truly impossible-stillness moments (smoke-column, dewdrops-held, cow's tail).
- **Bird-to-man transformation (1.13)** is the highest-risk shot in this act. First/last frame anchor strategy is essential. Budget multiple generation attempts.
- **The Geasa shot (1.15)** is a 40-second held shot — outside Veo's single-clip range. Strategy: generate one clean 8s base clip of Nemglan still, extend via held frames + crossfades in edit. The voice carries time; the visual carries weight.
- **Conaire stays silent throughout.** Any Veo generation of Conaire that produces lip movement during VO needs to be regenerated or have the audio of those moments handled to make clear the speech is not his.

### ElevenLabs voice notes

- **Narrator:** modern, plain, slight gravity. Capable of register-shift at Shot 1.19 — same voice, archaic delivery. Test: ask him to read both "There was a king in Erin once" and "Wind would not touch a beast's tail until nones" — the same voice should land both naturally.
- **Nemglan:** archaic, formal, neither warm nor cold. Slower than the narrator. Voice that sounds like it has been the same for a long time.

### Suno prompts (tentative)

- **"binding" track:** slow, formal, low strings or held drone with a single melodic figure that emerges and recedes. No percussion. Length: ~90 seconds, used sparingly across Movements 2–5. Reference: arvo pärt, *spiegel im spiegel*; not a copy, but the same family of restraint.

### Edit notes

- The held tone (low, sub-audible) is a single audio bed that runs from Shot 1.1 through Shot 1.21, ducked under music when music is present and surfacing when music is absent. It is the through-line of Act 1.
- Last frame of 1.21 holds for 1–2 seconds of black before the first frame of Act 2.
- File naming convention for production files: `da-derga/act-1/shot-1-XX-description.mp4`. Reference images: `da-derga/act-1/ref-X-description.png`.

## Open items raised by drafting

- **Three crowns (Shot 1.20):** literalizing the metaphor as actual woven crowns is a choice. Stokes's "three crowns on Erin" is metaphorical — the abundance itself is the crown. If literalizing feels heavy-handed in test, drop the shot and fold the line into 1.19 over the meadow image.
- **The silent shout (Shot 1.17):** untested. If Veo refuses or the silence reads as a technical glitch rather than a deliberate choice, fall back to ambient cheering at low level, preserving the directorial intent that Conaire never himself speaks.
- **Geasa shot held duration:** 40 seconds is the current target. If in test it cannot hold, fallback is to allow a single very slow zoom-in or change of light over the duration — minimal but enough to keep the eye. To be decided in production.
