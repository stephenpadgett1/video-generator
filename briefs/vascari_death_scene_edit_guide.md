# Outlaw Vampire Sommeliers — From Outer Space!
## Vascari's Death Scene — Edit Guide

### Project context

This is the final scene of a pulp/camp short film. Lord Vascari is an alien vampire aristocrat — a sommelier whose palate requires human blood — defeated on the bridge of his coffin-ship in orbit above Earth. He has just been stabbed through the chest with a corkscrew by Deputy Holloway, a 1950s lawman revived from cryosleep by the interstellar Protectorate to stop him. Ira, an alien vampire of Vascari's own kind who opposes the harvesting of human blood, watches in silence from the threshold of the bridge.

The scene is Vascari's death monologue. He sips his last glass of wine, delivers tasting notes on his killer, refuses to repent, and dies. The tone is camp-pulp — drive-in movie villainy played slightly straight, with one moment of genuine emotional weight at the climax.

Visual style throughout: stylized 2D comic-book animation. Flat color, heavy graphic shadows, dead black negative space, hard red emergency rim lighting. Aesthetic reference is roughly EC Comics pulp horror with Mike Mignola shadow work.

Target format: 9:16 vertical for social. Estimated final length: 60–90 seconds.

---

### Shot list

All clips were generated at 8 seconds in Veo. Some clips contain usable material across only part of their duration; trim freely. The shots are described in narrative order below.

---

#### 1. EXT — Coffin-ship in orbit (establishing)

The coffin-shaped vessel hangs above Earth, damaged, smoke trailing from a hull breach near the bridge. Earth fills the lower frame, dayside, blue atmospheric curve. Faint reds and yellows pulse from inside the breaches. The ship drifts. No camera movement.

**Use:** Opening shot of the piece. Establishes location, scale, and that the ship has already been crippled before we cut to the interior.

**Audio:** Deep low rumble + hiss of escaping atmosphere. No dialogue. May want a brief beat of pure space audio before any voice enters.

---

#### 2. INSERT — Corkscrew close-up

Tight on the corkscrew buried in Vascari's chest, against black silk waistcoat fabric. Single graphic line of dark red blood beneath. Dead black negative space. Held still life — no motion.

**Use:** Cut from the exterior establishing shot to this. Hold for ~2 seconds. The visual joke is the setup for Vascari's first line. Cut directly from this to Shot 4 (Vascari medium, "A corkscrew").

---

#### 3. Holloway — "It's over, Vascari."

Holloway in medium, weapon lowered, eyes off-camera right (locked on Vascari). Delivers the line flat, exhausted, no triumph. Holds afterward, watching for response.

**Use:** Could open the interior portion of the scene before Shot 4, or could be cut in after Shot 4 as part of the opening exchange. Either rhythm works:
- **Option A:** Corkscrew insert → Holloway "It's over" → Vascari "A corkscrew." (Holloway frames the situation before Vascari speaks.)
- **Option B:** Corkscrew insert → Vascari "A corkscrew" / "It is not fitting. It is cruel." → Holloway "It's over, Vascari" landing as confirmation. (Vascari leads, Holloway closes.)

Recommend Option A. The deputy delivering the verdict opens the scene with proper noir gravity, then Vascari's response undercuts it with absurdity.

---

#### 4. Vascari Shot 1 — "A corkscrew. It is not fitting. It is cruel. Quiet, deputy."

Vascari on throne, medium framing. Looks down at corkscrew, head lifts during the lines, settles into weary dismissal on "Quiet, deputy."

**Note:** Holloway's "Don't —" interjection scripted before "Quiet, deputy" was dropped from the Veo generation due to multi-voice issues. In the edit, "Quiet, deputy" can either:
- Stand alone as preemptive (Vascari sensing Holloway about to speak and silencing him before he can — stronger pulp choice)
- Have Holloway's "Don't —" added via ADR in post if desired

Recommend the preemptive read. Cut directly from Vascari's "It is cruel." to "Quiet, deputy" with no inserted Holloway.

---

#### 5. Holloway — "Seemed fitting."

Holloway in medium, eyes still locked off-camera right. Brief line, dry without being smug. Holds before and after.

**Use:** Cut here after Vascari's "It is cruel." The exchange is:
- Vascari: "A corkscrew."
- (beat)
- Vascari: "It is not fitting. It is cruel."
- Cut to Holloway: "Seemed fitting."
- Cut back to Vascari for "Quiet, deputy"

This means Vascari Shot 1 will need to be split across Holloway's insertion. The performance is continuous in the source clip but the edit interrupts it. Use Holloway as the cutaway during which Vascari's eyeline transitions from looking-down to looking-up.

---

#### 6. Vascari Shot 2 — "You came a very long way to kill a man you do not understand."

Vascari medium, looking left at Holloway. Eyeline shifts to right (toward Ira) at the end of the shot — discovery beat. Single line, then held discovery look.

**Use:** Vascari's first proper monologue line. The eyeline shift at the end establishes Ira's spatial position for the audience without showing her yet. Cut on the held look at the end.

**Note on eyeline shift:** Veo struggled with mid-shot eyeline changes. If the head turn looks rough, hide the transition by cutting away briefly during it — possibly to Holloway reaction or even a brief insert — and come back to Vascari already looking right.

---

#### 7. INSERT — Decanter and glass / hand grabs glass

Tight on the side table. Decanter and empty glass in still life. Vascari's hand enters frame and grabs the glass.

**Note:** The original concept was a full pour (decanter lifted, wine pouring into glass). The generation gave us only the hand grabbing the already-filled glass. This still works for the edit — the decanter is established, the ritual is suggested through hand presence, and the missing pour can be implied by an earlier cut.

**Voiceover during this insert:** "Earth. 2026. An unremarkable year, but the context —"

The line continues into Shot 8 where Vascari delivers "...is sublime."

**Use:** Cut here after Vascari's discovery look at end of Shot 6. The voiceover begins as the camera cuts away from his face. The hand entering frame is the visual rhythm. Hold long enough to establish the action, then cut to Shot 8 for "...is sublime" landing on his face.

---

#### 8. Vascari Shot 3c — Sip, savor, "...is sublime"

Vascari with glass already in hand. Raises to lips, sips, closes eyes for the savor (held — emotional center of the shot), opens eyes, turns head slightly left, delivers "...is sublime" with elegant finality.

**Use:** Cut from the decanter insert to this on Vascari's "...is sublime." The savor moment is the visual reward of the wine ritual. Don't rush it — hold on the closed-eye savor as long as the performance allows.

---

#### 9. Vascari Shot 4 — "Let me tell you what you taste like, deputy. I tasted you when you came aboard. Through the walls."

Vascari medium, head turned left to Holloway, glass held at chest level. Two-beat performance: warm hospitable setup ("let me tell you...") then confidential reveal ("I tasted you... through the walls"). Ends on knowing anticipatory smile.

**Use:** Direct cut from end of Shot 8. Vascari is settling into his tasting note. The end-of-shot smile is the bridge to Shot 10.

---

#### 10. Vascari Shot 5 — Tasting note + harvest correction

"Righteous. Thin. A little sour on the finish. Notes of cheap conviction. A man who has never once chosen the harder thing." [held silence — ADR insertion point for Holloway interjection] "I harvested, deputy. Please."

**Use:** Holloway's "You killed my wife" should be ADR'd into the held silence after "the harder thing." This is the audio sync from Holloway Shot 3 (see below).

The shot includes a natural pause after "harder thing" specifically to accommodate this. Listen for it.

---

#### 11. Holloway Shot 3 — "You killed my wife."

Holloway medium, jaw tight, knuckles white on the crossbow. Quiet, costing him. Held face after, working to recover.

**Use:** Cut here during Vascari Shot 5's held silence. The structure is:

- Vascari: "...notes of cheap conviction. A man who has never once chosen the harder thing."
- Cut to Holloway: "You killed my wife."
- Cut back to Vascari: "I harvested, deputy. Please."

Pacing on this is critical. Holloway's line should land in the held space Vascari left. The cut back to Vascari for "I harvested" is the moment of correction. Don't rush either side.

May be worth using extra footage from Holloway Shot 3 (before/after the line) as silent reaction beats elsewhere in the scene if rough cut reveals gaps.

---

#### 12. Vascari Shot 6 — "I have taken many wives, deputy. If yours was among them, she was exquisite. I remember them all."

Quietest Vascari shot of the scene. Soft, almost tender. The cruelest line delivered as warmth. Held expression at end carries strange tenderness.

**Use:** Cut from Vascari Shot 5's "I harvested, deputy. Please." into this. No reaction shot needed in between — let Vascari roll directly from correction to cruelty. The shift in his voice from controlled aristocratic correction to soft confidential almost-fondness is the entire performance.

If it lands too cold, consider cutting to a Holloway reaction (silent, from his Shot 3 outtakes) at any point during this shot. But ideally hold on Vascari uninterrupted — the absence of cutaway is what makes the cruelty land.

---

#### 13. Vascari Shot 7 — "They will say I was a criminal. They violated the Accords. They came to this vulgar little planet and took what was not theirs to take."

Vascari shifts from intimate to declamatory. Chin lifts, eyes go forward and slightly above camera. Speaking to history, not to the people in the room.

**Use:** Cut from the held tenderness of Shot 6 into this. The register change is dramatic — from whisper-cruel to public oratory. The audio shift carries the cut.

---

#### 14. Vascari Shot 8 — "And they will be right. And I regret nothing." (toast and drink)

Carries declamatory register, raises glass in toast, drinks (glass empties), lowers glass to side table, head comes back down, eyeline returns to Holloway on the left.

**Use:** Direct cut from Shot 7. The physical action — raising the glass, drinking, setting it down — is the bridge from the public declaration back to the private confrontation. By end of shot, Vascari is back on Holloway and ready for the next line.

If Veo's set-down was rough, a brief insert of the empty glass landing on the side table can be cut in to hide the moment.

---

#### 15. Vascari Shot 9 — "You may take my ship, deputy. You may take my life. And you, child — you may take my cape."

Hand returns to throne arm, both hands now on throne arms in original symmetric pose. Head turns from left (Holloway) to right (Ira) during the shot. Delivers "child" line in softer kin-register.

**Note on eyeline shift:** Veo's head turn was rough. Hide the transition by cutting away during the turn. See Shot 16 below.

---

#### 16. Ira reaction — cape line lands

Ira at threshold, hands folded, composed and unreadable. Eyes off-camera left (toward Vascari). Almost no motion — possibly a single slow blink.

**Use:** Cut here during Vascari Shot 9's head turn. The structure is:

- Vascari: "You may take my ship, deputy. You may take my life."
- Vascari head begins turning right
- Cut to Ira at the threshold (her shot is silent, ambient ship audio only)
- Vascari VO continues: "And you, child — you may take my cape."
- Hold on Ira's face as the line lands
- Optionally cut back to Vascari for end-of-shot held expression, or stay on Ira

**Important:** Ira's Veo generation included some unwanted elements — in one take she appears to deliver dialogue, in another there's a Vascari-sounding VO present. **Mute Ira's clip audio entirely.** Use the visual only. Vascari's cape line is sourced from Vascari Shot 9.

---

#### 17. Vascari Shot 10 — "But the hunger, friends. The hunger —"

Head returns to center. Eyes go forward, between Holloway and Ira. Performance falls away — aristocratic theatricality drops. Voice is quietest of the scene. Long held silence after "The hunger —" (em-dash). Ends mid-pause, before the verdict.

**Use:** Cut from end of Shot 9 (or from Ira's reaction shot, if held there) into this. The shot ends in held silence, which is the bridge to Shot 18.

---

#### 18. Vascari Shot 10b — CLOSE-UP — "I would starve for it once more."

**The only close-up in the scene.** Tight on Vascari's face. Eyes directly into camera (fourth-wall break). Single line delivered with absolute clarity. Hold afterward — eyes stay on camera. Faintest trace of a smile beginning at the very end.

**Use:** Cut here from end of Shot 10. The cut should happen during the held silence inside the em-dash — audience experiences the cut on stillness rather than on motion or dialogue, which gives it weight.

This is the rhetorical and emotional climax of the entire scene. Don't undercut it with a reaction shot. Hold on Vascari throughout. After the line, hold on his face as long as the performance supports.

---

#### 19. Vascari Shot 11 — Death

Cut back out to medium framing. Smile arrives — small, peaceful, fangs subtly visible. Held briefly. Then sharp cut as ship's emergency lighting goes out. Bridge plunges into near-darkness. Vascari's eyes close, head settles forward and slightly down. He dies.

**Use:** Direct cut from Shot 10b's held close-up to this medium. The shift in framing (close → medium) and the arrival of the smile are the visual transitions.

The lighting cut is in-shot — it's a hard cut from full red emergency to near-black. Don't dim it in post; the source clip should already do it. If Veo's transition was soft, sharpen it in post.

---

#### 20. Shot 12 — Glass coda

Cut from Vascari's death silhouette to a tight close-up on the empty wine glass on the side table. Near-darkness. A faint pulse of red light grows, catches the cut crystal in graphic highlights, holds, then fades. Glass returns to black. Cut to black at end of shot.

**Use:** Final shot of the scene. The cut from Shot 19 to this happens after Vascari has settled into death. The shot is the punctuation mark — the room remembering him for one last beat before going dark.

End the piece on the held black at the end of this shot. No additional exterior closer needed.

---

### Audio notes

- **Vascari's voice:** Veo generations gave us workable takes, but quality varies across shots. Plan for ElevenLabs ADR replacement on the most important lines, especially:
  - Shot 18 (close-up "I would starve for it once more") — must land perfectly
  - Shot 12 ("I have taken many wives") — quietest moment, voice precision matters
  - Shot 17–18 hunger couplet — pause inside em-dash needs to be held longer than Veo provided
- **Holloway's voice:** Generally usable across his three shots. Wife line may benefit from ADR for emotional precision.
- **Ira:** Silent throughout. Mute her clip audio entirely.
- **Multi-voice handling:** All cross-character interjections (Holloway "You killed my wife" landing in Vascari Shot 5's silence) need to be assembled in the audio mix from separate sources.
- **Ambient ship hum:** Should run continuously throughout the interior shots. The ship's audio dies during Shot 19 (death) — when emergency lights cut, the hum drops in pitch and fades. Shot 20 (glass coda) is near-silent. The exterior establishing (Shot 1) has its own bigger audio register (rumble + hiss); consider a brief audio match cut as we transition from exterior to interior.

### Pacing notes

Estimated runtime if all shots are used at recommended trim: 70–85 seconds. If tightening for sub-60-second cut, the most cuttable elements are:

- Shot 4 ("It is not fitting. It is cruel") — could trim "It is not fitting" line
- Shot 13 (declaration to history) — could trim one of the three "they" clauses
- Shot 14 (toast) — could shorten the physical action of glass-up to glass-down

Do NOT cut for time:

- Shot 18 (close-up) — needs full breathing room
- Shot 19 (death) — the lighting cut and settling beat
- Shot 20 (glass coda) — the quiet ending

### Final notes

The scene is built to escalate from camp to weight to silence. The opening is broad (corkscrew gag, deputy noir line). The middle is verbal showmanship (tasting notes, declamation). The climax is the close-up — the moment the camp drops away and Vascari is briefly serious. The death is fast, the coda is quiet. Trust the rhythm; don't over-cut.
