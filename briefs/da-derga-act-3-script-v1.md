# Da Derga's Hostel — Act 3 Shooting Script v1
## "The Arrivals"

---

## Act-level notes

**Estimated runtime:** 4:00 – 5:15. Cailb's name-litany is the longest single block of the piece so far.

**Aspect ratio:** 16:9 throughout — **with one possible exception.** Cailb's silhouette in the doorway (Shot 3.13) is the strongest candidate for a vertical break in the entire piece. I have drafted the shot in 16:9 with notes on how it could become vertical without rewriting. Decision deferred.

**Voices in this act:**
- **Narrator** (same voice as Acts 1–2). Minimal in this act; the doom-figures do most of the talking.
- **Doom-voice** (continues, still anonymous). Two Thread 1 placements.
- **Fer Caille** (debuts at the very first shot — continuation from Act 2's last frame). Recognition formula. Distinct voice from the doom-voice. A voice that sounds older than the man's body looks.
- **Da Derga** (one voice, brief). Welcomes Conaire. Warm, ceremonial. Mortal register — he is the only major host who is not also a doom-figure.
- **Cailb** (her single appearance — recognition formula, then 31-name litany in one breath). Woman's voice, on the edge of ecstatic recitation. Cast separately.
- **Conaire silent.** No dialogue. He admits Cailb against his geis with a gesture only.

**Music:**
- The pulse from Act 2 returns, slower, deeper. It is no longer an irregular thump but a steadier, slower rhythm — the heart of the Hostel itself, perhaps.
- Inside the Hostel, the pulse is layered with the sound of fire and the murmur of many voices, but the pulse is always present beneath.
- Under Cailb's litany: pulse drops away. Only her voice and a single sustained note.
- The spark moment (3.20–3.22) drops all music. The weapons fall in clean silence.

**Reference images required:**
- REF-A (Conaire) — locked.
- REF-L (Fer Caille) — established in Act 2.
- **REF-M:** Da Derga. A red-haired man, broad-shouldered, dressed in simple host's clothing. Warm-faced. Standing at the gate of the Hostel with torches lit behind him.
- **REF-N:** The Hostel exterior, full establishing shot. **The unrendered fifth reference from the seed.** Long timber structure at the edge of a plain. Seven visible doorways along the sides. The road of Cualu visibly enters one end and exits the other — the road runs *through* the building. Night. Torches lit along the walls. This is the production challenge of Act 3.
- **REF-O:** The Hostel interior. A great long hall. Long benches along the sides. Weapons in racks along the walls. A central hearth or multiple hearths. Torches. The road visible at the far end, a paler stripe of moonlight passing through.
- **REF #2 (Cailb at the doorway)** already established and approved.
- **REF-P:** Mac cecht. Conaire's champion. A large man, scarred, armored. Established here for visual; will be the central figure of Act 5.

**Continuity from Act 2:**
- Act 2 ends on Conaire's face, lit from below by the chariot lamp, turning toward Fer Caille.
- Act 3 opens on Fer Caille's mouth, opening to speak. No intervening cut.

## Movement 1 — Fer Caille speaks

The first recognition formula. Three shots.

### Shot 3.1
**Visual:** Tight on Fer Caille's mouth. Lit from below by the chariot lamp — same lamp that lit Conaire's face in 2.24. His mouth opens. Behind, the dark of the road.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** Silence. Then his voice — but the visual of the mouth opening precedes the sound by a beat. We see the breath drawn before we hear the words.

**Production notes:** This is a continuity-of-light shot from 2.24 — the lamp travels with us across the act break. If Veo struggles with the tight mouth shot, fall back to a slightly wider three-quarter face.

### Shot 3.2
**Visual:** Pull back. Fer Caille walking alongside the chariot, pig still under his arm (silent now), one eye on Conaire. He is speaking directly into the chariot.

**Duration:** 8s.

**Audio:** His voice. The pulse from Act 2 — slow, deeper now — returns under the words.

**VO (Fer Caille, on-screen, archaic):**
> *Welcome, O master Conaire!*
>
> *Long hath thy coming hither been known.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 37, verbatim. This is Thread 2 placement #1 — the doom welcomes the king.

**Production notes:** Fer Caille's voice should be older than his apparent age — a voice that has been waiting a long time. The two lines need clear separation; the welcome is a courtesy, the *"long hath thy coming been known"* is the doom underneath. Beat between them. The line is the foundational instance of Thread 2 — the only verbatim repetition of recognition that the piece carries cleanly (Cailb and Lomna's recognitions are different shapes, but Fer Caille's specifically is the canonical formula).

### Shot 3.3
**Visual:** Conaire's face — same lamp-lit face as 2.24, held a moment longer. He says nothing. He does not nod. His expression does not change.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Pulse. The pig stirs but does not squeal.

**VO:** None.

**Production notes:** Conaire's silence here is the established directorial choice continuing through the piece. The audience now expects he will not speak. They are correct.

## Movement 2 — The Hostel appears

The procession arrives. The Hostel is the visual problem of the act.

### Shot 3.4
**Visual:** Wide. The chariot, Fer Caille and Cichuil walking, and Conaire's retinue, cresting a low rise. Below them, in the middle distance, **the Hostel** — a long timber structure at the edge of a plain, lit from within. Torches along the walls. The road of Cualu enters one end of the building and exits the other; we can see the road continue out the far side.

**Duration:** 7s.

**Audio:** Pulse. The Hostel emits a low sound — many voices inside, a fire's roar — but it is distant from us still.

**VO (Narrator, returning for the first time in this act):**
> *And before them, in the valley, was the Hostel of Da Derga. The road of Cualu passed through its hall. The seven doorways were open.*

**Source:** Stokes pp. 39–40, compressed. Stokes describes the Hostel in detail across several pages; we compress to the three load-bearing facts: it is a hostel, the road passes through it, the doors are open.

**Production notes:** **REF-N. This is the production challenge of Act 3.** The road passing through the building was the unrendered fifth reference image. Strategy: Nano Banana for the wide establishing shot, treated as a still and panned/zoomed in edit, with Veo generating the surrounding movement (chariot cresting the rise, torchlight flicker). If Veo refuses or scrambles the geometry, the still is the canonical image and Veo handles only the foreground motion.

### Shot 3.5
**Visual:** Closer. The chariot moves toward the open gate. Da Derga himself (REF-M) stands at the threshold, red-haired, torches behind him. He raises a hand in welcome.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Pulse. Voices inside grow louder as we approach.

**VO (Da Derga, on-screen, warm and ceremonial):**
> *Welcome, my king. The Hostel is yours tonight.*

**Source:** Authored — Stokes's Da Derga has limited direct dialogue. The line is brief and serves to mark him as a *host*, distinct from the doom-figures around him. He is the only one in the piece who welcomes Conaire honestly.

**Production notes:** Da Derga's voice is the only fully *mortal* voice in this act. Distinct from Fer Caille's older-than-his-body register, distinct from Cailb's ecstatic recitation. He should sound like a man.

### Shot 3.6
**Visual:** Conaire dismounts. Wide of the entry. Fer Caille and Cichuil are at the edge of the procession, watching. They will enter too, but later, and from a different door. We do not see them enter.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Pulse. The interior is closer now — fire, voices.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *And Conaire entered, and his people with him, and the Hostel filled.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 41, compressed.

## Movement 3 — The Hostel fills

Compressed montage of arrivals inside. The chamber catalog of Act 4 will be the inverse of this — Ingcél scouting these same people from outside. We establish them here so the catalog can refer back.

### Shot 3.7
**Visual:** Interior wide. **REF-O.** Long hall, multiple hearths, torches, weapons on the walls in racks. The far end opens onto the moonlit road. Conaire takes his place at the central hearth. Around him, a great gathering — many figures, many seated, many standing.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** Pulse. Fire. Many voices, indistinct.

**Production notes:** This is the *positive* image of the Hostel — fullness, warmth, hospitality, kingship at center. Act 4 will systematically catalog these people from the destroyer's POV. The contrast must work both ways: this shot must read as warm, so Act 4's catalog reads as the warmth being inventoried for slaughter.

### Shot 3.8
**Visual:** Three quick establishing shots of specific figures who will recur:

(a) **Mac cecht** at his place — large, scarred, his great spear leaning against the bench beside him. He is alone among the gathering; he does not speak. **REF-P.**

(b) **Cormac Condlongas** — a warrior with his retinue around him, three nines of men (we see them as a group, not counted individually).

(c) **Lé fri flaith** — the boy from Act 2 (REF-H), seated by his father Conaire. He is not eating. He is looking at the door.

**Duration:** 4s each (12s total) — three brief inserts.

**Audio:** Pulse. Each insert is roughly silent — voices and fire continue underneath, but no one speaks.

**Source:** Stokes pp. 41–58, the great catalog of who is in the Hostel. We establish only the three who will matter most for the cascade: the champion (Mac cecht — water-quester in Act 5), the warrior whose retinue is fated to be torn apart (Cormac Condlongas), and the son who has already heard the doom-poem (Lé fri flaith).

**Production notes:** These are inserts, not full scenes. Each is a held composition, three to four seconds of held image with subtle motion — Mac cecht's spear leaning, Cormac's men talking among themselves, Lé fri flaith's face. Use the inserts to establish faces for Act 4's catalog. Each face must be re-renderable.

### Shot 3.9
**Visual:** Wide of the hall again. The doors begin to be closed — we see one of them, then another, swinging closed. Torchlight flickers. The road through the building is now visible only as a narrow line of moonlight where the gates have not yet shut.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** Pulse. The dull sound of heavy doors. The interior sound *deepens* as the doors close — voices and fire reverberate more.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *And the doors were closed against the night.*

**Source:** Stokes — there is no single line of this in the source; this is authored connective tissue. The closing of doors is implicit in the structure of the destruction-to-come.

**Production notes:** The closing of doors is a load-bearing structural beat. We see them closed here; in Act 5 we will see them broken open. The geometry of the road-through-the-building means the doors at the two ends matter most — but the side doors (the seven Stokes mentions) are also closed.

## Movement 4 — Cailb at the door

The verbal centerpiece of Act 3. The longest single block of voice in the piece so far.

### Shot 3.10
**Visual:** Exterior. Night now. The Hostel from outside, doors closed. A single figure approaches across the plain — a woman, walking. She is far away; her gait is slow and uneven. She does not carry a torch. She is finding her way by the light of the Hostel itself.

**Duration:** 8s.

**Audio:** Pulse, very low. Wind across the plain. The interior sounds are muffled now, behind the doors.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *After sunset, a woman came to the door of the Hostel. A lone woman after sunset. The king's tabu broken once more before she even spoke.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 81 — Cailb's arrival. The geis broken is *"After sunset, a company of one woman or one man shall not enter the house in which thou art."* We do not have the doom-voice quote the geis directly here (it is quoted by implication in the narrator's line) — saving the doom-voice for the Thread 1 placement after the litany.

**Production notes:** This is the slow approach. Cailb is a small figure on a wide plain. Her motion is wrong somehow — not limping, but *off*. Veo: prompt for an unnaturally uneven gait, never specified in detail. Keep her small and distant; she will not become large until the doorway shot.

### Shot 3.11
**Visual:** Interior. A torch on the wall by the main door gutters. One of the figures near the door — an unnamed retainer — turns his head toward it. The pulse is audible inside now.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** Pulse. The torch's sputter.

### Shot 3.12
**Visual:** The main door, from inside, beginning to open. A vertical sliver of night appears.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** Pulse. The door's hinge.

### Shot 3.13 *(Possible vertical-aspect break)*
**Visual:** **REF #2 — established.** Cailb at the threshold. Doorway frames her. She is in the heron-posture: one foot down, one foot up against her thigh, one arm raised, one arm against her body, head turned in profile, eyes elsewhere. Behind her, the night and the empty plain. In front of her, the firelit interior.

The composition is symmetrical and architectural — the figure dead center, the doorway framing exactly.

**Duration:** 7s.

**Audio:** Pulse drops out entirely. A single sustained tone enters — high, thin, single note. Wind. Her breath.

**Production notes:** This is the strongest vertical-aspect candidate in the piece. The composition is already vertical-friendly — a tall doorway with a tall figure inside it. **If we commit to a vertical break here**, the shot becomes 9:16 for its duration (Shots 3.13 through 3.18, the entirety of Cailb's appearance), then returns to 16:9 at 3.19 (the door closing behind her). This would also be the moment the audience feels the piece *change shape* to honor a moment of recognition. The case against: the whole piece has been 16:9, and breaking aspect ratio is a strong reach. **Recommendation: drafted as 16:9 for now, but the production decision should be made early — Cailb's reference image (REF #2) is already 16:9 and would need to be regenerated in 9:16 if we commit to the break.**

If 16:9: keep the symmetrical doorway composition, but allow more of the interior firelight to spill around her, giving the frame visual weight on both sides.

### Shot 3.14
**Visual:** Hold on Cailb. Same posture. She has not moved. The hall behind has fallen silent — the murmur dies.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** The single tone. Her breath. Nothing else.

**VO (Cailb, on-screen, ecstatic recitation register):**
> *I see for thee, O king, that neither fell nor flesh of thine shall escape from the place into which thou hast come, save what birds will bear away in their claws.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 82, verbatim. This is Thread 2 placement #2 — the doom names the king's death. Recognition as judgment.

**Production notes:** Cailb's voice for the first time. This recognition formula needs to land before the litany — it is the verdict; the litany is the signature on the verdict. Ecstatic recitation means: not theatrical, not screamed, but possessed. The voice is hers and not hers. ElevenLabs casting: search for a voice that can do trance-like delivery without artificial reverb. The reverb is the room and her body and the doorway, not an effect.

### Shot 3.15
**Visual:** Cailb. Still in the heron-posture. The Hostel still silent.

**Duration:** 50s.

**Audio:** The single sustained tone. The litany delivered against it.

**VO (Cailb, on-screen, in a single sustained breath — performance illusion to be constructed in ElevenLabs):**
> *Cailb!*
>
> *Samon, Sinand, Seisclend, Sodb, Saiglend, Samlocht,*
>
> *Caill, Coll, Díchóem, Díchuil, Díthrub, Dáire, Dárine, Der, Egem, Agam, Ethamne, Gním, Cluiche, Cethardam, Nith, Nemain, Nóennen, Badb, Blosc, Bloar,*
>
> *Huae, óe Aife la Sruth, Mache, Médé, Mod.*

**Source:** Stokes p. 82, verbatim. Cailb's thirty-one (or thirty-two, depending on parse) names. The source's stage direction is also verbatim and load-bearing: *"On one foot, and holding up one hand, and breathing one breath she sang all that to them from the door of the house."*

**Production notes:** **This is the longest verbal block of the piece so far.** Performance design:

- ElevenLabs cannot deliver a literal 50-second single breath. The *illusion* of the single breath is what we are constructing. Strategy: deliver the litany at a consistent rapid cadence, with no audible breath intakes between names. If ElevenLabs produces audible breaths, they should be edited out in post.

- The names should not be delivered all at the same pace. Stokes's text has commas and groupings; the grouping suggests phrasing. The blocks I have separated above are *suggestions* for phrasing groups within the one-breath illusion — slight increases in pace between groups, like a runner finding a rhythm.

- The visual is a single held image of Cailb for the full duration of the litany. Same problem as the geasa shot (1.15) — 50 seconds is far outside Veo's single-clip range. Strategy: a high-quality Nano Banana still treated as a held frame, with subtle Veo-generated motion layered (a slight flicker of torchlight from inside, a slight movement of her cloak's edge in the night wind). Her body itself does *not* move — the heron-posture is the binding-made-visible, structurally analogous to Nemglan's stillness in 1.15.

- If the voice cannot hold across 50 seconds without breath-pauses being audible, fall back to two breaths — one for the first group ("Cailb! Samon, Sinand…"), one for the rest. The illusion is weaker but defensible. Stokes's "one breath" was always a performance claim, not a physiological one.

### Shot 3.16
**Visual:** Conaire's face, lit by the interior firelight, watching. His mouth does not move. His expression does not change — but his eyes do. He sees what is at his door.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Cailb's litany completing. The sustained tone holds a moment longer, then fades.

**Production notes:** Conaire's silent recognition. The audience knows: he knows. He is about to admit her against his geis.

### Shot 3.17
**Visual:** Conaire raises his hand. The gesture of admission. Slow. Final.

**Duration:** 3s.

**Audio:** Silence. The tone is gone. The pulse has not returned yet.

**Source:** Stokes p. 83 — Conaire admits Cailb against his geis because she has named her own admission as already-decided. He cannot refuse her without violating the law of hospitality, which is a higher law than his geis. He chooses to break the geis rather than refuse the threshold.

This is one of the most painful moments in the source — Conaire's tragedy is that he is *good*, and his goodness is what destroys him.

### Shot 3.18 *(Thread 1 placement #1 of Act 3)*
**Visual:** Cailb enters. We see her cross the threshold — one step. Then the doorway is empty for a beat — she has passed out of frame into the interior. The night plain visible beyond the open door.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** The pulse returns. Slow. Heavy. One slow beat across the duration of the shot.

**VO (Doom-voice):**
> *Clouds of weakness are coming to you.*

**Source:** Spine doc Thread 1 placement.

**Production notes:** First Thread 1 placement of Act 3 — and the audience has now heard this line four times across Acts 2 and 3. By this point the line carries the weight of pattern. It is no longer ambient prophecy; it is a verdict being entered into a ledger.

The empty doorway after Cailb crosses is the load-bearing image — the threshold has been violated, and we are looking at the violation in plain view. The night beyond the door reads, suddenly, as *outside*. We have been inside the Hostel for the entire act. The outside now exists.

### Shot 3.19
**Visual:** The main door closing. From inside, the same angle as 3.12 but in reverse — the sliver of night narrows, then is gone.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Pulse. The hinge. The interior sound returns — voices, fire — but the murmur is muted now, uncertain.

**VO (Narrator):**
> *And the door was closed behind her.*

**Source:** Authored.

**Production notes:** The bookend to 3.12. Door opened (Cailb arrives), door closed (Cailb is inside). Structurally rhymes the closing of doors in 3.9 — but where 3.9 was protective ("closed against the night"), 3.19 is sealing-in. The doom is inside now.

## Movement 5 — The spark

The act ends with Mac cecht's gesture. The first sign from inside the Hostel that registers outside — a bridge to Act 4.

### Shot 3.20
**Visual:** Mac cecht (REF-P). Close. He is by the wall, near the weapons rack. A torch beside him has gone out. He picks up his spear, casually, and strikes the iron of its blade against the stone of the wall.

**Duration:** 4s.

**Audio:** Pulse. The room sounds. His footstep.

**Production notes:** The gesture is *ordinary*. A man relighting a torch. He has done this a thousand times. The spark is small; he is not casting a spell. The doom is what *follows* the small ordinary act.

### Shot 3.21
**Visual:** Close on the spear-blade striking stone. A single bright spark leaps from the impact.

**Duration:** 2s.

**Audio:** The strike. A single clear ringing sound — much louder than the gesture should have produced.

**Production notes:** The audio is the cue, not the visual. The strike of metal on stone produces a ringing that is somehow far too large for the room. Sound design: the strike, then a half-second of ringing decay that extends beyond what the visual would suggest.

### Shot 3.22
**Visual:** The weapons in the rack on the opposite wall. Quickly, one by one, they fall — first one spear, then another, then a sword from the wall, then several at once, clattering to the stone floor.

**Duration:** 5s.

**Audio:** The ringing decay continues underneath. Each weapon falling — *clang, clang, clang* — but the falls are out of rhythm with each other, an irregular percussion. The pulse is gone.

**Source:** Stokes — the weapons falling from racks is a *portent* in the source, occurring more than once in the narrative as a sign of impending destruction. We compress to one clear instance, attached to Mac cecht's spark.

**Production notes:** Veo strategy: this is achievable as a single held shot of a weapons rack with weapons falling at controlled intervals. Generate as 4-5 second clip with explicit prompt for *sequential* falling (not simultaneous). If Veo defaults to all-at-once collapse, generate three separate clips of one or two weapons falling each, splice in edit with audio carrying continuity.

### Shot 3.23 *(Thread 1 placement #2 of Act 3)*
**Visual:** The last weapon on the floor. The room is silent. Nobody in the Hostel is looking at the rack — the gathering continues, oblivious. Only Mac cecht stands by his torch (now lit), looking at the fallen weapons.

**Duration:** 6s.

**Audio:** Clean silence. Then — for the first time — a sound that is *not* coming from inside the Hostel. A sound that is *outside*. Distant. The shifting of bodies on a far shore. The grind of a boat-keel against shingle. Not yet identified for the audience, but unmistakably *exterior*.

**VO (Doom-voice):**
> *Clouds of weakness are coming to you.*

**Production notes:** Second and final Thread 1 placement of Act 3 — the fifth across the piece so far. The line lands not in silence (as in 3.18) but against the first external sound the audience hears since the doors closed. The exterior is *audible*. Something is gathering out there.

The doom-voice has now spoken the line five times. Act 4 will reveal who has been speaking.

### Shot 3.24
**Visual:** Wide of the Hostel interior. The gathering continues, fire, voices — the warmth of Movement 3 has resumed, but the audience now knows what is sitting in the doorway-shaped silence. Cailb is somewhere in the hall (we do not see where). The fallen weapons are at the wall. Mac cecht stands.

The image holds for a long beat, then begins, very slowly, to pull back — the camera retreats through the doorway we have just spent the act inside. The doors close in front of us. We are outside.

**Duration:** 8s.

**Audio:** Interior sounds receding. The exterior sounds rising — distant shore, water, voices that are not in the Hostel. Wind.

**Production notes:** This is the bridge to Act 4. The camera leaves the Hostel by passing through the door we entered through — a reverse of 3.4–3.6. By the end of the shot, we are outside, looking at the Hostel from a middle distance, and the sounds we are hearing belong to whatever is offscreen on the far side of the plain.

The shot ends on the exterior view of the Hostel, doors closed, light spilling from the seven doorways — and somewhere offscreen, but audibly close, a hundred men gather.

Cut to black. Act 4 opens on Ingcél's face.

## Production appendix

### Veo prompting principles for Act 3

- **The Hostel exterior (3.4, REF-N).** The unrendered fifth reference from the seed. The geometry — road passing through building — is the production challenge. Strategy: Nano Banana for the canonical wide still, treating the geometry as architectural rather than physical (the road doesn't have to be drivable; it just has to be visible passing through). Veo handles foreground motion only. **Test this image first** before committing to any other Act 3 production work; if the road-through-building cannot be rendered convincingly, the entire act's establishing geography needs to be reconsidered.

- **The Hostel interior (3.7, REF-O).** Multiple hearths, long benches, weapons racks, road visible at the far end. Achievable but complex. Recommend generating the still and using it as a held image with Veo handling specific inserts (fire flicker, individual figures in motion).

- **Cailb at the doorway (3.13, REF #2 established).** This is the strongest single image we have. The heron-posture must be exactly right — one foot up against the thigh, one arm raised straight. Veo will try to relax the posture; do not let it. If Veo cannot hold the pose across the held duration, treat the Nano Banana still as canonical and use it as a held frame with minimal Veo overlay (torchlight flicker, edge of cloak).

- **Cailb's litany (3.15).** 50 seconds of held image. Same strategy as 1.15 and 2.17 — Nano Banana still, extended with Veo-generated atmospheric motion. The visual is *not* allowed to move; her body holds the posture.

- **Weapons falling (3.22).** Sequential, not simultaneous. Watch Veo's instinct toward dramatic en-masse collapse. Prompt explicitly for staggered falls with irregular intervals.

- **Camera retreating through the doorway (3.24).** A specific camera move — pulling back through an architectural threshold. Veo can handle this if prompted as a tracking shot. If unreliable, achieve by cross-cutting between an interior wide and an exterior wide with audio carrying the continuity.

### Voice notes

- **Fer Caille.** Older than his body. The two-line recognition formula needs a clear beat between courtesy and doom.
- **Da Derga.** Mortal, warm, ceremonial. The only fully mortal voice in this act. He is the only character in the piece who welcomes Conaire honestly.
- **Cailb.** The performance challenge of the piece. Trance-recitation register without theatrical excess. The 50-second illusion of single-breath delivery is constructed in post if necessary.
- **Doom-voice continues.** Two placements in Act 3. By the end of Act 3 the line has been delivered five times across the piece; the audience now expects it.

### Suno / music notes

- The pulse from Act 2 returns with different character — slower, deeper, more measured. Less the irregular thump of escalating cascade, more the *heart of the Hostel itself*. Suno prompt direction: "slow heartbeat, deep, even, measured. No melody. The sound of a room that is itself alive."

- The single sustained tone under Cailb (3.13–3.16) is distinct from any other audio bed in the piece so far. High, thin, single note. Could be Suno-generated or built in production with a held synth tone.

- Music drops entirely under the spark sequence (3.20–3.23). Silence is the music there.

### Edit notes

- The doors closing in 3.9 and 3.19 should sound *exactly the same* — same audio sample, two instances. The first closes the night out; the second closes the night in.
- The exterior sounds rising in 3.23 and 3.24 are the audience's first auditory contact with what is coming. They should be specific: water, footsteps on shore, the grind of beached boats, the voices of many men too low to parse. This is the sound bed Ingcél will inhabit in Act 4.
- Act 3 ends on the exterior view of the Hostel. Hold 2 seconds before cut to black. Act 4 opens on Ingcél's face.

## Open items raised by drafting

- **Vertical-aspect break at Shot 3.13.** Strongest candidate in the piece. Recommendation deferred to production; if committed, Cailb's reference image needs to be regenerated in 9:16, and the litany shot needs to be authored as vertical from the start. The case for is the formal honoring of a moment of recognition; the case against is breaking the 16:9 commitment of the piece. **Open.**

- **Cailb's litany delivery — one breath illusion vs. two.** The 50-second single breath is a performance claim from Stokes, not a physiological fact. ElevenLabs may not be able to deliver convincingly in one continuous take. The fallback (two breaths, grouped) is defensible but loses some of the source's load. To be tested.

- **Da Derga's only line.** Authored, not from Stokes. If this feels like a betrayal of the source-integrity principle, can be replaced with a wordless welcome gesture and the line dropped. The risk: without his voice, Da Derga becomes anonymous, and the *one* mortal welcome of the act loses its weight.

- **The fallen weapons remaining where they fell vs. being cleaned up.** I have left them on the floor through 3.24, structurally — they are still there for Act 4's catalog when Ingcél peers in through the door-cracks. Whether this geography is honored depends on Act 4's blocking. If Act 4 is staged differently, the weapons can be picked up and replaced in 3.23 with the same audio cue but a different visual outcome.

- **Whether we ever see Cailb again after she enters.** I have made her invisible-after-entry in 3.24 — she has passed into the hall and we do not see where she sat. The alternative is to show her at the hearth, integrated into the gathering. The invisibility reads as more haunting; the visibility is more textually faithful (Stokes does describe her place inside the hall). Open.
