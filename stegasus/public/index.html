Steganography as a service

Every video we publish carries sixteen bytes of audio steganography in its soundtrack — a tiny message hidden in the noise floor, inaudible to a listener, recoverable by anyone with an audiowmark decoder.

One slot per video. Yours to fill. You write the sixteen bytes — a name and a date, a coordinate, a hash, a quiet line for someone who knows to look. We embed them. They go out with the next video and live there forever.
